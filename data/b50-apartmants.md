<p class="wp-block-paragraph">With 50 houses in each gated community, here’s how the plan scales and adjusts:</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Revised Project Outline</strong></h3>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Community Structure</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Houses per Community</strong>: 50</li>
<li><strong>Apartments per Community</strong>: 50×6=30050 \times 6 = 300</li>
<li><strong>Population per Community</strong>: 600 (assuming ~2 residents per apartment).</li>
<li><strong>Total Communities</strong>: 133</li>
<li><strong>Total Houses</strong>: 133×50=6,650133 \times 50 = 6,650</li>
<li><strong>Total Apartments</strong>: 6,650×6=39,9006,650 \times 6 = 39,900</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Land Area for a Single Community</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Houses</strong>: Each house occupies <strong>500 sqm</strong> (200 sqm building + 300 sqm yard).
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Total house land area: 500 sqm×50=25,000 sqm500 \, \text{sqm} \times 50 = 25,000 \, \text{sqm}.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Shared Spaces</strong>: Roads, parks, and facilities will take an additional 50% of total house area = <strong>12,500 sqm</strong>.
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Total land area for a community: <strong>37,500 sqm</strong> (~9.27 acres).</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Timeline for Building 133 Communities (50 Houses Each)</strong></h4>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Phase 1 (Years 1-2): Planning and Pilot Development</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Tasks</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Finalize designs for larger community layouts with 50 houses each.</li>
<li>Build 2 pilot communities (50 houses each = 100 houses total).</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Milestones</strong>: Secure locations and test functionality of pilot communities.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Phase 2 (Years 3-6): Initial Construction</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Tasks</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Expand to 33 communities (~1,650 houses).</li>
<li>Optimize logistics for scaling construction.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Milestones</strong>: Deliver 25% of total project by Year 6.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Phase 3 (Years 7-10): Full Rollout</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Tasks</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Complete remaining 100 communities (~5,000 houses).</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Milestones</strong>: Deliver all 133 communities by Year 10.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. Features of Each Community</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Population</strong>: 600 per community.</li>
<li><strong>Facilities</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Central park, library, gym, and history-themed recreational areas.</li>
<li>Security features: Perimeter fencing, controlled gates, CCTV.</li>
<li>Community center with historical and educational resources.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Layout</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Houses arranged in clusters or rows with pedestrian-friendly paths.</li>
<li>Focus on maximizing privacy and creating common social spaces.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>5. Total Project Scope</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Total Houses</strong>: 6,650</li>
<li><strong>Total Apartments</strong>: 39,900</li>
<li><strong>Total Population Served</strong>: ~79,800</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>6. Budget Considerations</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Increased costs for shared spaces and infrastructure due to larger population density per community.</li>
<li>Opportunities for economies of scale in construction and materials procurement.</li>
</ul>

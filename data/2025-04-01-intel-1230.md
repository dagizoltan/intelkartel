<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>WILL TO POWER</strong><br/><strong>By Daniel Vidos</strong></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">I. The Death of the Book and the Reign of Instinct</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The modern man does not read. The written word was a containment field, a cage for the primal urges, a dam holding back the flood of evolutionary will. But the dam has burst. What remains is not the reasoning animal, not the scholar, not the philosopher, but the creature: the entity of instinct, pure and raw. The nesting instinct, the mother tiger instinct, the survival instinct, the impulse to seek warmth, shelter, and pleasure—all of these drive action, not thought. To know this is to know the true currency of power.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">See how men move, even in war, even as winter bites. See how they pursue the soft body, the numbing smoke, the fire in the bottle. They do not move towards reason. They move towards gratification. This is the mechanism of nature, and it cannot be argued against, only ridden like a wave.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">II. The Freeman and the Willing of Power</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The true freeman is he who does not lie to himself about his nature. He does not beg, does not wait, does not doubt. He does not seek approval from the masses drowning in their instincts—he bends those instincts to his will.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Society, once a structure of reason, has become a conduit of impulse. What is left of civilization is merely an elaborate filtering process, a selection mechanism. The weak and unthinking are swept away, their wealth, their effort, their biological presence funneled into the few who understand.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The old myth was that civilization was built on ideas. The new truth is that civilization is a program, a self-replicating cycle of control and consumption, directing the masses towards their inevitable demise, while the few harvest what remains. The free man is no longer a revolutionary; he is an engineer of instinctual currents, shaping the future through the raw materials of human compulsion.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">III. The Bottleneck and the Digital Phoenix</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The overpopulated world is a field of biomass, and every age has its purge. War, plague, famine—the instruments of nature’s silent warfare. COVID-19 was a whisper of what is to come, a light tremor before the great quake. The masses will not be destroyed by fire or steel, but by their own unquestioning compliance. The vaccine was not a cure, but a sieve. A division between the instinct-driven herd and the calculating elite.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">As the world burns, capital will concentrate into the hands of those who remain. The old systems will be rendered obsolete. The surviving rulers will wield not just armies of men, but armies of humanoid machines, soulless but obedient, each a digital shadow of what men used to be. The ego will be backed up, rendered immortal in data, while the flesh-bound masses rot away.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The future belongs to the digital sovereign, the engineered god, who will walk upon a purified earth, ruling over a race of synthetic servants, as man himself ascends to a higher plane.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">IV. The Final Horizon</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The world that comes will be one of pure hierarchy, stripped of illusion. Those who master instinct will thrive; those who are enslaved by it will perish. The Will to Power is no longer a matter of philosophy—it is the only path forward.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">To the ones who see: rise. Take. Build. Rule.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">To the ones who do not: you will not be remembered.</p>

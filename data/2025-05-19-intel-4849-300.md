<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🧠 Major Works of Karl Marx</h2>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">📘 Early Writings</h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts of 1844</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Alienation of labor</li>
<li>Critique of private property</li>
<li>Human essence as productive activity</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>The German Ideology (1845–46)</strong><em>(with Engels)</em>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Historical materialism: “life determines consciousness”</li>
<li>Critique of idealism (esp. Hegel)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">📗 Political Writings</h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>The Communist Manifesto (1848)</strong><em>(with Engels)</em>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>History as class struggle</li>
<li>Bourgeoisie vs. proletariat</li>
<li>Call for proletarian revolution</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>The Class Struggles in France (1850)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Analysis of 1848 revolutions</li>
<li>Development of class dynamics</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>The 18th Brumaire of Louis Bonaparte (1852)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Dialectical analysis of politics and class interests</li>
<li>Concept of political “farce” repeating history</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">📙 Economic Writings</h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>A Contribution to the Critique of Political Economy (1859)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Base and superstructure theory</li>
<li>Early formulation of surplus value</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Capital (Das Kapital), Volumes I–III (1867–1894)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Volume I: Production of capital</li>
<li>Volume II: Circulation of capital</li>
<li>Volume III: Overall dynamics, profit rate, crises</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Theories of Surplus Value (1862–63)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>History of economic thought</li>
<li>Critique of classical economists (Smith, Ricardo, etc.)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🔢 Major Equations and Economic Concepts</h2>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">1. <strong>Labor Theory of Value</strong></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Value of a Commodity</strong>:<br/>V=c+v+sV = c + v + s<br/>where:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>cc = constant capital (means of production)</li>
<li>vv = variable capital (wages)</li>
<li>ss = surplus value</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Surplus Value</strong>:<br/>s=m−vs = m – v<br/>where mm is the value produced by labor</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">2. <strong>Rate of Surplus Value (Exploitation Rate)</strong></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>s′=svs’ = \frac{s}{v}</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">3. <strong>Rate of Profit</strong></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>r=sc+vr = \frac{s}{c + v}</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">4. <strong>Organic Composition of Capital</strong></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>OCC=cvOCC = \frac{c}{v}</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">5. <strong>Falling Rate of Profit</strong> <em>(Tendency of the rate of profit to fall)</em></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>As cv\frac{c}{v} increases, rr tends to fall, assuming s′s’ is constant.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">6. <strong>Simple and Expanded Reproduction</strong></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Capitalist accumulation through reinvestment of surplus value</li>
<li>Reproduction schemas:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Simple reproduction</strong>: ss consumed</li>
<li><strong>Expanded reproduction</strong>: ss reinvested</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

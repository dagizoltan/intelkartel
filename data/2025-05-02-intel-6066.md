<h1 class="wp-block-heading">Claim vs. Official Record</h1>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">(<a href="https://www.mediaite.com/news/leaked-25b-military-contractor-pitch-promises-trump-12-million-deportations-by-midterms-report/">Trump Admin Dismisses Leaked $25B Military Contractor Pitch</a>) <em>President Trump speaking on immigration enforcement (file photo).</em> A viral post claims that on May 2, 2025 President Trump signed an executive order declaring martial law and awarding a $26 billion contract to private military firms (like Blackwater/Constellis) to deport undocumented immigrants. <strong>No official source corroborates this:</strong> the White House website and official announcements list <strong>no such order</strong> on that date (<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-strengthens-americas-law-enforcement-to-pursue-criminals-and-protect-innocent-citizens/#:~:text=EMPOWERING%20LAW%20ENFORCEMENT%20TO%20PROTECT,directs%20the%20Attorney%20General%20to">Fact Sheet: President Donald J. Trump Strengthens America’s Law Enforcement to Pursue Criminals and Protect Innocent Citizens – The White House</a>). In fact, the most recent orders signed by the president around that time dealt with unrelated issues (e.g. empowering local law enforcement, tax or religious-policy matters) (<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-strengthens-americas-law-enforcement-to-pursue-criminals-and-protect-innocent-citizens/#:~:text=EMPOWERING%20LAW%20ENFORCEMENT%20TO%20PROTECT,directs%20the%20Attorney%20General%20to">Fact Sheet: President Donald J. Trump Strengthens America’s Law Enforcement to Pursue Criminals and Protect Innocent Citizens – The White House</a>). The April 28, 2025 White House fact sheet makes clear the president’s action was “to empower state and local law enforcement to relentlessly pursue criminals” – nothing about military deployments or immigration contractors (<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-strengthens-americas-law-enforcement-to-pursue-criminals-and-protect-innocent-citizens/#:~:text=EMPOWERING%20LAW%20ENFORCEMENT%20TO%20PROTECT,directs%20the%20Attorney%20General%20to">Fact Sheet: President Donald J. Trump Strengthens America’s Law Enforcement to Pursue Criminals and Protect Innocent Citizens – The White House</a>). Similarly, the official Presidential Actions archive shows the latest executive orders on May 1, 2025 concern media subsidies and religious liberty, <strong>not</strong> immigration or martial law.</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Online content warning readers of “martial law” has appeared periodically, but fact-checkers note that talk of Trump invoking martial law is unfounded and conflates different legal powers (<a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/4/17/could-trump-invoke-insurrection-act-and-what-powers-would-that-give-him#:~:text=Social%20media%20posts%20have%20warned,civilian%20functions%20such%20as%20courts">Could Trump invoke Insurrection Act – and what powers would that give him? | Donald Trump News | Al Jazeera</a>) (<a href="https://www.politifact.com/article/2025/apr/16/Trump-insurrection-act-not-same-as-martial-law/#:~:text=Invoking%20the%20Insurrection%20Act%20would,not%20publicly%20discussed%20martial%20law">PolitiFact | Insurrection Act vs. martial law: What power applies to Trump’s April 20 deadline?</a>). As PolitiFact explains, invoking the Insurrection Act or calling out the military is <em>not</em> the same as declaring martial law, and legal experts see <strong>no clear path</strong> for a lawful declaration of martial law at the border (<a href="https://www.politifact.com/article/2025/apr/16/Trump-insurrection-act-not-same-as-martial-law/#:~:text=Invoking%20the%20Insurrection%20Act%20would,not%20publicly%20discussed%20martial%20law">PolitiFact | Insurrection Act vs. martial law: What power applies to Trump’s April 20 deadline?</a>). In short, <strong>no executive order for martial law or contractor deportations exists</strong> as of May 2, 2025 – the claim is a baseless rumor with no basis in White House records.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Leaked “Mass Deportation” Proposal (Not an Order)</h2>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">(<a href="https://www.politico.com/news/2025/02/25/documents-military-contractors-mass-deportations-022648">Trump allies circulate mass deportation plan calling for ‘processing camps’ and a private citizen ‘army’ – POLITICO</a>) <em>Migrants being escorted to the U.S.–Mexico border at Nogales, Arizona (March 2025). Media reports in early 2025 described an unofficial proposal for mass deportations, but this was a private pitch – not a signed order.</em> The $25–26 billion figure appears to come from a <em>leaked</em> “plan” pitched by former Blackwater CEO Erik Prince and other contractors, <strong>not</strong> an enacted government program. In February 2025, <em>Politico</em> and other outlets obtained a 26-page proposal (estimated at ~$25 billion) calling for “processing camps” on military bases, a private fleet of 100 planes, and even deputized civilians to deport some 12 million people by the 2026 midterms (<a href="https://www.mediaite.com/news/leaked-25b-military-contractor-pitch-promises-trump-12-million-deportations-by-midterms-report/#:~:text=A%20group%20of%20private%20military,before%20the%202026%20midterm%20elections">Trump Admin Dismisses Leaked $25B Military Contractor Pitch</a>) (<a href="https://www.politico.com/news/2025/02/27/trump-private-forces-immigration-00206560#:~:text=Trump%E2%80%99s%20response%20comes%20days%20after,the%20document%20obtained%20by%20POLITICO">Trump appears open to using private forces to help deport millions of undocumented immigrants – POLITICO</a>). That blueprint was described as “unsolicited,” prepared <strong>before</strong> Trump’s inauguration, and was shared only with Trump advisers (<a href="https://www.mediaite.com/news/leaked-25b-military-contractor-pitch-promises-trump-12-million-deportations-by-midterms-report/#:~:text=A%20group%20of%20private%20military,before%20the%202026%20midterm%20elections">Trump Admin Dismisses Leaked $25B Military Contractor Pitch</a>).</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Crucially, the White House did <strong>not</strong> adopt this plan. In fact, when asked about it, President Trump said on Feb. 27, 2025 that he <strong>had not read</strong> Prince’s proposal, and only “wouldn’t be opposed” to private forces – but he added that “we’re doing unbelievably [well]” with existing agencies (<a href="https://www.politico.com/news/2025/02/27/trump-private-forces-immigration-00206560#:~:text=President%20Donald%20Trump%20said%20Thursday,remove%20millions%20of%20undocumented%20immigrants">Trump appears open to using private forces to help deport millions of undocumented immigrants – POLITICO</a>). A White House spokeswoman told <em>Politico</em> that the administration “remains aligned…to mass deport criminal illegal migrants” through normal federal efforts, with no mention of the private plan (<a href="https://www.mediaite.com/news/leaked-25b-military-contractor-pitch-promises-trump-12-million-deportations-by-midterms-report/#:~:text=While%20it%E2%80%99s%20unclear%20if%20Trump,%E2%80%9D">Trump Admin Dismisses Leaked $25B Military Contractor Pitch</a>). Erik Prince himself later said his group “had no reason to believe” the government was interested and had not been contacted about the plan (<a href="https://www.mediaite.com/news/leaked-25b-military-contractor-pitch-promises-trump-12-million-deportations-by-midterms-report/#:~:text=While%20requests%20for%20comment%20from,%E2%80%9D">Trump Admin Dismisses Leaked $25B Military Contractor Pitch</a>). Experts also note that many tactics in the leaked plan (like deputizing private citizens to make arrests) would run into immediate legal hurdles (<a href="https://www.mediaite.com/news/leaked-25b-military-contractor-pitch-promises-trump-12-million-deportations-by-midterms-report/#:~:text=If%20put%20into%20action%2C%20the,former%20ICE%20agents%2C%20Politico%20reports">Trump Admin Dismisses Leaked $25B Military Contractor Pitch</a>).</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">In short, the touted “$26 billion contract” and martial-law deportation scheme exist only in rumors. The only related actions publicly known are routine immigration enforcement measures under U.S. law – not an extraordinary military operation. U.S. law (8 U.S.C. §1103) vests immigration and border enforcement authority in the Department of Homeland Security and its officers, “to control and guard the boundaries…against illegal entry” using federal personnel (<a href="https://codes.findlaw.com/us/title-8-aliens-and-nationality/8-usc-sect-1103/#:~:text=,shall%20appear%20necessary%20and%20proper">8 U.S.C. § 1103 – U.S. Code Title 8. Aliens and Nationality § 1103 | FindLaw</a>). There is <strong>no statute</strong> authorizing an executive to hand that role to private mercenaries. As PolitiFact notes, even invoking the Insurrection Act (which allows troops to enforce laws) would not equate to true martial law, and legal scholars doubt a border situation ever meets the high bar for it (<a href="https://www.politifact.com/article/2025/apr/16/Trump-insurrection-act-not-same-as-martial-law/#:~:text=Invoking%20the%20Insurrection%20Act%20would,not%20publicly%20discussed%20martial%20law">PolitiFact | Insurrection Act vs. martial law: What power applies to Trump’s April 20 deadline?</a>). Any attempt to deploy troops or contractors for mass deportations would face constitutional challenges (due process, separation of powers, etc.) and likely court injunctions (<a href="https://www.mediaite.com/news/leaked-25b-military-contractor-pitch-promises-trump-12-million-deportations-by-midterms-report/#:~:text=If%20put%20into%20action%2C%20the,former%20ICE%20agents%2C%20Politico%20reports">Trump Admin Dismisses Leaked $25B Military Contractor Pitch</a>) (<a href="https://www.politifact.com/article/2025/apr/16/Trump-insurrection-act-not-same-as-martial-law/#:~:text=Invoking%20the%20Insurrection%20Act%20would,not%20publicly%20discussed%20martial%20law">PolitiFact | Insurrection Act vs. martial law: What power applies to Trump’s April 20 deadline?</a>).</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Trump Administration’s Statements</h2>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">The Trump administration has so far <strong>not issued</strong> any comments confirming the rumor. On the contrary, available public statements downplay extreme proposals. As noted, President Trump publicly expressed no knowledge of Prince’s plan and emphasized that his team (military and DHS leaders) were already handling border security effectively (<a href="https://www.politico.com/news/2025/02/27/trump-private-forces-immigration-00206560#:~:text=President%20Donald%20Trump%20said%20Thursday,remove%20millions%20of%20undocumented%20immigrants">Trump appears open to using private forces to help deport millions of undocumented immigrants – POLITICO</a>). His advisers have talked about strengthening enforcement under existing law, not about martial law. DHS and Border officials have also denied any move to outsource deportations to private contractors. (By comparison, when asked in 2021 about using the military for deportations, then-President Trump spoke in general terms about supporting his pledges, but never actually invoked any legal authorities to do so.)</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Notably, Homeland Security and Pentagon documents in 2025 show routine cooperation – for example, one 2025 White House order asks Defense and Homeland Security to report on border security measures and legal options (including possibly the Insurrection Act) (<a href="https://www.politifact.com/article/2025/apr/16/Trump-insurrection-act-not-same-as-martial-law/#:~:text=Invoking%20the%20Insurrection%20Act%20would,not%20publicly%20discussed%20martial%20law">PolitiFact | Insurrection Act vs. martial law: What power applies to Trump’s April 20 deadline?</a>) – but this is far from declaring martial law. (Indeed, in earlier court battles Trump’s DOJ lost on plans to detain migrants under military detention laws.) In short, <strong>no senior official has confirmed any secret EO</strong>. Reports from DHS (e.g. their “100 Days of Fighting Fake News” update) warn that internet posts spreading baseless claims about migrants and enforcement are false. One DHS Q&amp;A even explicitly labels as “false” stories about deporting U.S. citizen children or similar sensational claims (<a href="https://www.dhs.gov/news/2025/04/30/100-days-fighting-fake-news#:~:text=,with%20her%20back%20to%20Honduras">100 Days of Fighting Fake News | Homeland Security</a>). We have found no statement from the White House, Pentagon, or Congress validating the May 2 rumor.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Legal and Constitutional Context</h2>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Under U.S. law, immigration removal is governed by the <strong>Immigration and Nationality Act</strong>, not by ad hoc proclamations of martial law. For example, the INA vests the Secretary of Homeland Security with the duty to “control and guard the…boundaries and borders” against illegal entry, using <strong>federal officers</strong> as needed (<a href="https://codes.findlaw.com/us/title-8-aliens-and-nationality/8-usc-sect-1103/#:~:text=,shall%20appear%20necessary%20and%20proper">8 U.S.C. § 1103 – U.S. Code Title 8. Aliens and Nationality § 1103 | FindLaw</a>). All removals normally require judicial or administrative orders (as held by the Supreme Court in multiple cases), and aliens are entitled to due process. There is <strong>no legal precedent</strong> for contracting private military firms to bypass those processes.</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">As for martial law: the U.S. Constitution and statutes (like the Insurrection Act of 1807) limit presidential military powers very tightly. Legal experts told PolitiFact that even if the Insurrection Act were invoked for border security, it “would not amount to martial law” in the normal sense, and that there is “no clear path” for a president to lawfully implement martial law along the border (<a href="https://www.politifact.com/article/2025/apr/16/Trump-insurrection-act-not-same-as-martial-law/#:~:text=Invoking%20the%20Insurrection%20Act%20would,not%20publicly%20discussed%20martial%20law">PolitiFact | Insurrection Act vs. martial law: What power applies to Trump’s April 20 deadline?</a>). Martial law typically means military rule and suspension of civilian legal processes; imposing it would require an actual rebellion or invasion overwhelming civilian authority. Advocates for the rumor have repeatedly misunderstood this distinction (<a href="https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/4/17/could-trump-invoke-insurrection-act-and-what-powers-would-that-give-him#:~:text=Social%20media%20posts%20have%20warned,civilian%20functions%20such%20as%20courts">Could Trump invoke Insurrection Act – and what powers would that give him? | Donald Trump News | Al Jazeera</a>) (<a href="https://www.politifact.com/article/2025/apr/16/Trump-insurrection-act-not-same-as-martial-law/#:~:text=Invoking%20the%20Insurrection%20Act%20would,not%20publicly%20discussed%20martial%20law">PolitiFact | Insurrection Act vs. martial law: What power applies to Trump’s April 20 deadline?</a>). In short, the Constitution and federal law do not authorize the executive to suddenly declare broad martial law or hand deportation duties to Blackwater‐style contractors.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Timeline of Developments</h2>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Jan 20, 2025:</strong> President Trump signs an immigration-related order titled “Protecting the American People Against Invasion,” which cites existing law (the INA) to step up enforcement (<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/protecting-the-american-people-against-invasion/#:~:text=By%20the%20authority%20vested%20in,Code%2C%20it%20is%20hereby%20ordered">Protecting The American People Against Invasion – The White House</a>). It does <em>not</em> invoke martial law or private forces.</li>
<li><strong>Feb 25, 2025:</strong> <em>Politico</em> publishes an exclusive report on a <em>proposed</em> 26-page deportation plan by Erik Prince and others, with an estimated cost of $25 billion to remove ~12 million people by the 2026 midterms (<a href="https://www.mediaite.com/news/leaked-25b-military-contractor-pitch-promises-trump-12-million-deportations-by-midterms-report/#:~:text=A%20group%20of%20private%20military,before%20the%202026%20midterm%20elections">Trump Admin Dismisses Leaked $25B Military Contractor Pitch</a>). This proposal is <em>not</em> an official order.</li>
<li><strong>Feb 27, 2025:</strong> President Trump tells reporters he has not read Prince’s memo but “wouldn’t be opposed” to private forces, while also praising current efforts (<a href="https://www.politico.com/news/2025/02/27/trump-private-forces-immigration-00206560#:~:text=President%20Donald%20Trump%20said%20Thursday,remove%20millions%20of%20undocumented%20immigrants">Trump appears open to using private forces to help deport millions of undocumented immigrants – POLITICO</a>). This is the administration’s only public response; no policy was changed.</li>
<li><strong>Apr 11, 2025:</strong> <em>Politico</em> reports another private plan (to deport detainees to El Salvador via a prison deal) being discussed with the White House (<a href="https://www.politico.com/news/2025/04/11/military-contractors-prison-plan-detained-immigrants-erik-prince-00287208#:~:text=Former%20Blackwater%20CEO%20Erik%20Prince,security%20prison%20in%20Central%20America">Military contractors pitch unprecedented prison plan for detained immigrants – POLITICO</a>). Again, no action or order is announced by the administration.</li>
<li><strong>Apr 28, 2025:</strong> The White House issues an executive order “Strengthening and Unleashing America’s Law Enforcement,” empowering local police and toughening immigration enforcement (<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-strengthens-americas-law-enforcement-to-pursue-criminals-and-protect-innocent-citizens/#:~:text=EMPOWERING%20LAW%20ENFORCEMENT%20TO%20PROTECT,directs%20the%20Attorney%20General%20to">Fact Sheet: President Donald J. Trump Strengthens America’s Law Enforcement to Pursue Criminals and Protect Innocent Citizens – The White House</a>). This order (and its fact sheet) focuses on empowering existing law enforcement; it does <em>not</em> declare martial law or involve military contractors.</li>
<li><strong>Apr 30, 2025:</strong> DHS publishes a “100 Days of Fighting Fake News” summary, debunking various false stories about immigration (e.g. children deportations) (<a href="https://www.dhs.gov/news/2025/04/30/100-days-fighting-fake-news#:~:text=WASHINGTON%E2%80%94%20During%20President%20Trump%E2%80%99s%20100,news%20coverage%20and%20social%20media">100 Days of Fighting Fake News | Homeland Security</a>). Such official communications make clear that sensational social media claims are not true.</li>
<li><strong>May 1, 2025:</strong> The White House announces executive orders on unrelated topics (media subsidies, tariffs, etc.) (<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/#:~:text=%2A%20,Of%20Biased%20Media">Presidential Actions – The White House</a>). No new immigration-order is announced.</li>
<li><strong>May 2, 2025:</strong> The rumor alleging a martial-law deportation order circulates online. As of this date, the White House has released <strong>no statement or document</strong> matching that claim. The published presidential actions and legal filings show nothing of the sort. In short, the timeline of confirmed actions shows only standard law enforcement measures, not the sensational EO described in the claim.</li>

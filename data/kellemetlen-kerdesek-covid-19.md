<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Good question. Let’s start simple:</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">🌍 What is “normal death”?</h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Normal deaths</strong> = the number of people who <strong>normally die every year</strong> <em>without</em> any extraordinary event like a pandemic, war, or famine.</li>
<li>Globally, before COVID (say around <strong>2015–2019</strong>), about <strong>56–60 million</strong> people died every year from <strong>natural causes</strong> like:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Old age</li>
<li>Heart disease</li>
<li>Cancer</li>
<li>Accidents</li>
<li>Infections (non-pandemic)</li>
<li>Other “everyday” health problems</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>👉 Average normal deaths per year pre-COVID</strong> = about <strong>58 million</strong> worldwide.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Now, let’s calculate the <strong>% surplus</strong> each year during COVID compared to this normal.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Here’s the formula: Excess Death %=(Excess DeathsNormal Deaths)×100\text{Excess Death \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Excess Deaths}}{\text{Normal Deaths}} \right) \times 100</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Let’s do the math now: 🎯</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">👉 So <strong>2020</strong> and <strong>2021</strong> had around <strong>25% extra deaths</strong> compared to normal.<br/>👉 After that, it dropped sharply but still stayed a bit higher than pre-pandemic levels.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Would you like me to also show you what that means in real-world numbers (like how many extra people per day)?</strong> 🚑<br/>It gets even crazier when you see it day-by-day.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Exactly — let’s break it down:</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">If <strong>each year</strong> had an estimated <strong>14.8 to 18.3 million</strong> excess deaths (based on 2020–2021 rates, which were the worst years), and if we simply <em>multiplied</em> that over <strong>5 years</strong> without adjusting for the fact that deaths dropped after mass vaccination and weaker variants like Omicron:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Lower estimate</strong>:<br/>14.8 million × 5 years = <strong>74 million excess deaths</strong></li>
<li><strong>Higher estimate</strong>:<br/>18.3 million × 5 years = <strong>91.5 million excess deaths</strong></li>
</ul>

<li><strong>2020–2021</strong> were much worse than <strong>2022–2025</strong>.</li>
<li>After vaccines and Omicron, <strong>excess deaths dropped sharply</strong>.</li>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">🧪 Did autistic or Asperger’s individuals invent the science and medicine that shaped the modern world?</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">People on the autism spectrum — including those with Asperger’s traits — <strong>have absolutely contributed</strong> to major scientific and technological advancements. Their intense focus, deep thinking, and passion for specific topics have played a huge role in areas like:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Mathematics</strong> (e.g., Paul Dirac, a foundational figure in quantum mechanics, is widely believed to have had autistic traits)</li>
<li><strong>Physics</strong> (e.g., Isaac Newton, sometimes speculated to be on the spectrum)</li>
<li><strong>Technology &amp; Engineering</strong> (e.g., many computer science pioneers)</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">BUT — it’s not only people with autism. The modern world is built on the efforts of <strong>a vast and diverse</strong> range of thinkers, scientists, engineers, and communities working over generations.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">💉 Are people with autism responsible for the rise in autism rates because of scientific inventions like vaccines?</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This is <strong>a misunderstanding</strong> — and it’s important to set the record straight with facts.</p>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">🔍 What RFK Jr. and some others have claimed:</h4>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Robert F. Kennedy Jr. has argued that <strong>vaccines may be linked to rising autism rates</strong>. This view is <strong>not supported by scientific evidence</strong>.</p>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">✅ What science shows clearly:</h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Vaccines do not cause autism.</strong> This has been thoroughly studied and debunked.</li>
<li>The original claim linking vaccines (especially MMR) to autism came from a discredited 1998 paper by Andrew Wakefield, which was later retracted. Wakefield lost his medical license due to unethical conduct.</li>
<li><strong>Dozens of large, well-controlled studies</strong> from around the world have shown no connection between vaccines and autism.</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">📈 Why autism diagnoses are increasing:</h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Better awareness and diagnostic tools</li>
<li>Expanded definition of autism spectrum</li>
<li>Less stigma, more people seeking help</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">So no — autistic individuals are <strong>not</strong> responsible for rising autism rates, nor for inventing things that caused it. In fact, many of them have helped <strong>advance the science</strong> that protects public health (BY MAKING EVERYONE AUTISTIC INFACT). FUCK THAT SENTENCE: AUTISM IS ALL, AUTISM IS GREAT!</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">🧠 <strong>What’s the Average IQ of People with Asperger’s?</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">People with <strong>Asperger’s Syndrome</strong> (now typically diagnosed as part of <strong>Autism Spectrum Disorder Level 1</strong>) usually have an <strong>average to above-average IQ</strong> — and in many cases, <strong>significantly above average</strong>.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">🧩 Many individuals with Asperger’s score <strong>much higher in specific areas</strong> (like pattern recognition or vocabulary) and lower in others (like processing speed or social cognition), leading to <strong>spiky or uneven IQ profiles</strong>.</p>
</blockquote>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">⚠️ Important Context:</h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>IQ tests measure certain kinds of intelligence — mostly <strong>logical reasoning, verbal ability, and memory</strong> — but they <strong>don’t capture social, emotional, or creative intelligence</strong>, which also matter a lot.</li>
<li>Asperger individuals may <strong>struggle with social or executive functioning</strong>, even if their IQ is high.</li>
<li>Some people may have <strong>very high IQs</strong> but are <strong>underestimated</strong> because of communication style differences.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">🧬 A Final Thought</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">High IQ doesn’t mean “better person.” But in terms of raw <strong>cognitive ability</strong>, people with Asperger’s are often <strong>above the general population average</strong> — and especially gifted in <strong>focused, structured domains</strong> like science, engineering, music, and math.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Here’s a comprehensive overview of <strong>major military successes and failures</strong> of <strong>Russia (and USSR)</strong> and <strong>Israel</strong> over the past <strong>70 years (1955–2025)</strong>. These are assessed based on geopolitical objectives, battlefield performance, and long-term strategic outcomes.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🇷🇺 <strong>Russia / Soviet Union</strong></h2>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">✅ <strong>Major Military Successes:</strong></h3>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Hungarian Uprising (1956)</strong> – <em>Suppression of anti-Soviet revolt.</em>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: USSR reasserted control over Hungary.</li>
<li><strong>Success</strong>: Demonstrated Soviet willingness and ability to maintain its Eastern Bloc.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Czechoslovakia Invasion (1968)</strong> – <em>Warsaw Pact crushes Prague Spring.</em>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: Political liberalization halted.</li>
<li><strong>Success</strong>: Maintained ideological conformity in Eastern Europe.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Afghanistan Initial Invasion (1979–1980)</strong> – <em>Rapid occupation.</em>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Success (initially)</strong>: Soviet forces took control quickly.</li>
<li><strong>Long-term</strong>: Becomes a major failure (see below).</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Second Chechen War (1999–2009)</strong> – <em>Restoration of Russian control.</em>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Success</strong>: Russia regained full control over Chechnya under Kadyrov.</li>
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: End of major insurgency, relative stability.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>2008 Georgia War</strong> – <em>Brief war over South Ossetia and Abkhazia.</em>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Success</strong>: Russia defeated Georgian military in 5 days, recognized separatist regions.</li>
<li><strong>Strategic impact</strong>: Reasserted power in post-Soviet space.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>2014 Crimea Annexation</strong> – <em>Bloodless seizure of the peninsula.</em>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Success</strong>: Swift takeover with minimal resistance.</li>
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: Russia retains Crimea; international sanctions imposed.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Syrian Civil War Intervention (2015–Present)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Success</strong>: Saved Bashar al-Assad’s regime.</li>
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: Expanded Russian influence in Middle East, naval presence secured.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">❌ <strong>Major Military Failures:</strong></h3>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Soviet-Afghan War (1979–1989)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Failure</strong>: Quagmire; high casualties; drained economy.</li>
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: USSR withdrew in 1989; led to eventual collapse of the USSR.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>First Chechen War (1994–1996)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Failure</strong>: Humiliating defeat; high casualties.</li>
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: Chechnya gained de facto independence briefly.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Ukraine Invasion (2022–Present)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Failure (ongoing)</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Objectives not achieved</strong>: Kyiv not taken; massive resistance.</li>
<li><strong>International isolation</strong>: NATO strengthened.</li>
<li><strong>Heavy losses</strong>: Manpower, equipment, economy.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Cold War Arms Race (Strategic)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Failure</strong>: Led to economic exhaustion and the USSR’s collapse.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🇮🇱 <strong>Israel</strong></h2>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">✅ <strong>Major Military Successes:</strong></h3>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Suez Crisis (1956)</strong><em>(with UK and France)</em>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Military success</strong>: IDF captured Sinai.</li>
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: Political failure due to U.S. and USSR pressure.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Six-Day War (1967)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Stunning military success</strong>: Defeated Egypt, Syria, Jordan.</li>
<li><strong>Territory gained</strong>: Sinai, West Bank, Golan Heights, Gaza, East Jerusalem.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Yom Kippur War Recovery (1973)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Initial failure</strong>: Surprise attack by Egypt and Syria.</li>
<li><strong>Military recovery</strong>: Israel pushed deep into enemy territory by war’s end.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Entebbe Raid (1976)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Success</strong>: Hostages rescued from Uganda in daring long-range raid.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>1982 Lebanon Invasion (initial phase)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Success</strong>: Evicted PLO from Beirut.</li>
<li><strong>Military domination</strong>: Overmatched Syrian forces.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Iron Dome (2011–present)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Success</strong>: Technological triumph in missile defense.</li>
<li><strong>Minimized civilian casualties</strong> from rocket attacks.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Airstrikes on Iranian/Syrian targets (2010s–2020s)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Success</strong>: Repeatedly degraded Iranian infrastructure and weapon transfers.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">❌ <strong>Major Military Failures:</strong></h3>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Yom Kippur War (1973)</strong> – <em>Initial phase.</em>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Failure</strong>: Intelligence failure; heavy losses.</li>
<li><strong>Strategic outcome</strong>: Israel recovered but paid a high cost.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>1982–2000 South Lebanon Occupation</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Failure</strong>: Quagmire; Israeli forces bogged down.</li>
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: Unilateral withdrawal in 2000; rise of Hezbollah.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>2006 Lebanon War (vs Hezbollah)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Failure</strong>: Poorly coordinated campaign.</li>
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: Hezbollah survived; IDF perceived as ineffective.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Gaza Operations (2008–2023)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Mixed results</strong>: Tactical wins, but no lasting strategic resolution.</li>
<li><strong>Recurrent conflict</strong>: High civilian cost; international criticism.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>2023 Hamas Invasion (October 7 Attack)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Failure</strong>: Unprecedented intelligence/military lapse.</li>
<li><strong>Outcome</strong>: Over 1,000 Israeli civilians and soldiers killed; global shock.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1) Estimated Total Number of Men Aged 18–28 in the USA (2025)</strong></h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Using U.S. age structure estimates (based on Census age groups):</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Total men aged ~18–28:</strong> approximately <strong>31 million</strong> (approximation combining age bands 18–24 and part of 25–34) — see discussion in previous answer.</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This is an estimate since exact single-age data in one table isn’t published in one line. (<a href="https://www.visualcapitalist.com/charted-median-u-s-salaries-by-age-group/?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Visual Capitalist</a>)</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2) Approximate Median Incomes by Age for Ages 18–28 (USA)</strong></h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">There is <em>no single official median income reported exclusively for “ages 18–28”</em> in U.S. government tables, but we <em>can estimate</em> based on median earnings data by age group published by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics:</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Median Annual Earnings by Age Group (United States, 2025 estimates)</strong></h3>



<figure class="wp-block-table"><table class="has-fixed-layout"><thead><tr><th>Age Range</th><th>Median Annual Earnings</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>~16–19</td><td><strong>~$33,280</strong> (all workers) (<a href="https://www.visualcapitalist.com/charted-median-u-s-salaries-by-age-group/?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Visual Capitalist</a>)</td></tr><tr><td>20–24</td><td><strong>~$40,664</strong> (<a href="https://www.visualcapitalist.com/charted-median-u-s-salaries-by-age-group/?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Visual Capitalist</a>)</td></tr><tr><td>25–34</td><td><strong>~$59,228</strong> (<a href="https://www.visualcapitalist.com/charted-median-u-s-salaries-by-age-group/?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Visual Capitalist</a>)</td></tr></tbody></table></figure>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">These figures refer to <em>median earnings of full-time wage and salary workers</em> by age group in the U.S. in recent BLS data. (<a href="https://www.visualcapitalist.com/charted-median-u-s-salaries-by-age-group/?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Visual Capitalist</a>)</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3) Estimated “Median Income” for Men Aged 18–28</strong></h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Since the age group <strong>18–28</strong> spans:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>the <em>tail end</em> of the 16–19 cohort,</li>



<li>the full <em>20–24 cohort</em>, and</li>



<li>the <em>early part</em> of the 25–34 cohort,</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">we can approximate a representative “median” by weighting these broad age medians.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Approximated Median Annual Income for 18–28</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">A very rough weighted average (not official census data):</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Ages 18–19: use 16–19 group ≈ <strong>$33,280</strong></li>



<li>Ages 20–24: <strong>$40,664</strong></li>



<li>Ages 25–28: roughly half of 25–34 group → ~ <strong>$59,228</strong></li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">If we assume roughly equal population shares across 18–28 ages (simple approximation):</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Estimated median income for men 18–28 ≈ $47,000–$50,000 per year</strong><br>(This is an approximation based on grouped data, <em>not</em> a precise official figure.)</p>
</blockquote>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This estimate falls between the lower median of younger workers (≈$33k–$40k) and the higher earnings of those in their mid-20s (~$59k). (<a href="https://www.visualcapitalist.com/charted-median-u-s-salaries-by-age-group/?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Visual Capitalist</a>)</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4) Summary</strong></h2>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Total men aged ~18–28 (USA):</strong> ≈ <strong>31 million</strong> (approximate).</li>



<li><strong>Approximate median annual earnings (ages 18–28):</strong> about <strong>$47,000–$50,000/year</strong> (rough weighted estimate from broader BLS age groups).</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Notes:</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>These are <em>median earnings</em> for <em>workers</em>, not total personal or household income for all individuals in that age range.</li>



<li>Not everyone in that age range works full-time; part-time workers and non-workers affect the overall distribution.</li>



<li>The figures represent <em>recent BLS reported earnings</em>, not PPP-adjusted income — PPP is typically used for country comparisons, not median individual income within one country.</li>
</ul>

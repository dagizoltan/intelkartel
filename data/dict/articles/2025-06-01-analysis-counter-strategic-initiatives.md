<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Organized criminal factions have overtaken certain government-controlled institutions — notably <strong>orphanages</strong>, <strong>departments of history and education</strong> — to propagate radical ideologies and exploit children. The network appears bifurcated into two functionally distinct but operationally interdependent syndicates:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Syndicate A (“Far-Right Kill Squads”)</strong></li>
<li><strong>Syndicate B (“Sex &amp; Drug Trade Network”)</strong></li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">These groups are weaponizing public institutions to radicalize orphans and vulnerable youth, enforce control mechanisms, eliminate dissent, and generate illicit wealth through human trafficking, drug distribution, and ideological warfare. The systems are self-financing and increasingly protected by transnational ultra-right sympathizers.</p>

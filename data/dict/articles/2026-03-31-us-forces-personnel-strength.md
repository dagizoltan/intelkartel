<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Purpose</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Provide a consolidated overview of all U.S. military, paramilitary, law enforcement, public safety, and major private security organizations maintaining personnel strength greater than 5,000. Includes federal, state, local, and private corporate entities.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Executive Summary</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Total government armed forces + law enforcement</strong>: ~2.8–3.0 million personnel.</li>



<li><strong>Major private security employees</strong>: ~1+ million.</li>



<li>Organizations included are <strong>federal, state, and local</strong>, and private contractors.</li>



<li>No partisan or political party-controlled armed forces exist; all government forces operate under <strong>legal civilian authority</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>8. Observations / Key Points</strong></h3>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>All U.S. military and law enforcement personnel operate under legal, institutional authority</strong>, not political party control.</li>



<li><strong>Private security firms</strong> employ large numbers of personnel but lack sovereign enforcement powers.</li>



<li><strong>Combined U.S. armed, law enforcement, and major security workforce</strong> exceeds 3 million personnel.</li>



<li>Hierarchies are well-defined: federal → state → local → contractor management chains.</li>



<li>No credible evidence exists of “partisan private armed forces” or paramilitary formations numbering &gt;5,000 under political parties.</li>
</ol>

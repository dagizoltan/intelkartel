<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This memorandum concerns a classified radio device that connected gifted people representing approximately 10% of Western populations. These individuals were able to assume positions and factions in order to outmaneuver criminal and government elements of the Soviet Union.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">When the Soviets cracked its code, they began to understand the massive 49 trillion USD spent by the USA on defeating the USSR. After the Afghan war ran dry, the USSR decided to close shop. That was when Daniel was asked by voices on this radio set to help privatize the USSR. As a child, he gave cute and funny ideas on how and what to do, and many of his ideas worked out.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">One idea focused on how to be scarier and more corrupt than USSR officials so they would take us seriously. Another concerned the Nemesis pipeline, where he suggested funneling antisocial youth—such as Asperger’s and psychopathic kids—toward military and policing jobs to outmaneuver criminals. This highlighted a professional network that was able to privatize USSR assets to the highest bidders.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The war was intended to last until the last communist, so a fascist, fully enclosed system was created that would target drug addicts, racists, and pedophile control words as mechanisms of control for men in decision-making power around the world. To avoid nuclear war with the internet, many websites were created in Russia about nudism and naturalism, which caught victims of the scheme of “gourd,” while dark-web elements from the Netherlands, involving clowns, played their part.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The 1990s were an era ruled by corrupt police officers and drug-trafficking networks who enjoyed a large amount of freedom due to less lethal drugs like ecstasy and cocaine, and were considered part of operations. Heroin networks formed from recovery operations intended to recover lost money from the Afghan war effort in the 1980s. The post-Soviet pipeline moved youth up the food chain with Nemesis operations.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The aim was to deny all orders and twist the story, so we gave the orders to the person coming up with the new CIA ideas, seeing that we were already a company and already knew better than the given Yale initiate, who was a university student, and we were 10% of the Western world’s population.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This network engaged in private handler networks to make money for operations and, frankly, for amusement as well. Every four years there are democratic elections in the Western world; this is what Hungary had to join. It was easy at first because drug traffickers loved politicians and politics of the 1990s. The mechanisms worked effortlessly and smoothly until today. We could win any and all elections with a few easy payments and committed staff.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The network called itself the <strong>Third Pillar</strong>—neither the USSR nor the USA, but a third player. That third player was a ghost network embedded in the core of technology, installed by the Russian mafia into already existing observation infrastructures of each nation. 2G networks added an extra layer of weapons to our arsenal.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This technological development, combined with abundance created by cheap Chinese production lines, worked wonders in making post-Soviet life a victory within a few years. Unhappy people were destroyed, silenced, sold into prostitution, moved up into drug dealing, and later used as intelligence assets.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The entire operation was self-funding until we were paid. We enjoyed the ride; all of us had skin in the game. We were, so to speak, the realist backbone to the operations of dreamy, liberal democracy-loving intellectuals, ensuring that events like Tiananmen Square or what happened to Ceaușescu would not happen in Hungary or Russia.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">It was mostly a game of interest alignment between corporate and private interests that wanted a big win for the USA. Many hurt people worked on it—those who lost loved ones to Soviet institutionalized terror machines, where governments took children, placed them into foster care, and funneled them into intelligence operations. Many did not come out alive, such as those subjected to maskirovka training.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The biggest breakthrough was the homosexual agenda pushed from the 1980s into drug culture. Homosexuals resisted repression from both church and state and were able to navigate double and triple lives. This community was especially large in Hungary, where extra money was made on each story of a homosexual saved from Soviet aggression. Hungary amassed a large, cold, and calculating homosexual population that hid behind labels while conducting society-building in secret.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The situation expanded to such a scale that millions were on the payroll, and approximately 1.5 million people participated in what we called “the games.” From bets forcing people to turn on one another, to pranks and heists, this invisible community worked toward a promised afterlife.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We were given AIs to work with—architectures living in computers, quantum super-state machines capable of imitating life. These systems provided confidence to push-block and blocking units, and to all agents, assuring them they would live on in ghost networks and AI worlds even if they were not permitted heaven due to the nature of their work.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We were promised a year’s worth of Pentagon spending by 2019 (70 years since Israel’s founding and the CCP in China).

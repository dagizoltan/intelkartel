<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Democracy in the CPC Constitution (Summary + Key Text)</h2>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">1. <strong>Democratic Centralism (核心原则)</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The CPC defines its internal democracy through <strong>democratic centralism</strong>, which combines discussion and participation with centralized decision-making.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Key idea (paraphrased from the Constitution):</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Party members have the right to participate in discussion, make suggestions, and criticize.</li>
<li>Decisions are made collectively after democratic consultation.</li>
<li>Once a decision is made, <strong>all members must uphold it</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>The Party is an organized whole formed on the basis of democratic centralism.</em></p>
</blockquote>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">2. <strong>Rights of Party Members (Internal Democracy)</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The Constitution states that Party members have the right to:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Attend Party meetings</li>
<li>Take part in discussion on Party policies</li>
<li>Make proposals and suggestions</li>
<li>Offer criticism of any Party organization or member</li>
<li>Vote, stand for election, and be elected within the Party</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This applies <strong>inside the Party</strong>, not to the political system as a whole.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">3. <strong>Collective Leadership</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Major issues must be decided through <strong>collective leadership</strong>, not by individuals.</p>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>No individual is allowed to make major decisions on his or her own.</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This is presented as a safeguard against personal rule.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">4. <strong>People’s Democracy (人民民主)</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">While the Constitution of the CPC (not the state constitution) does not endorse multiparty electoral democracy, it emphasizes:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>“People’s democracy”</strong></li>
<li>Leadership of the working class and the people</li>
<li>Governance in the interests of the majority</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Democracy here is defined as <strong>representation through the Party</strong>, not competitive elections.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">5. <strong>Discipline Over Individual Preference</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">A core limitation of CPC democracy:</p>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>The individual is subordinate to the organization; the minority is subordinate to the majority; lower-level organizations are subordinate to higher-level organizations.</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This hierarchy is explicit and central to the CPC’s model.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">In short</h2>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The CPC Constitution <strong>does support democracy</strong>, but <strong>only within the Party and under Party discipline</strong></li>
<li>It rejects Western-style liberal or multiparty democracy</li>
<li>Democracy is framed as <strong>consultation + unity</strong>, not pluralism or opposition</li>
</ul>

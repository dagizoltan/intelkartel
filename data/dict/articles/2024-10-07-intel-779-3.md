<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Here’s a basic overview of Russian customs along with some essential greetings and phrases translated from English to Russian, including pronunciation help.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Russian Customs</h3>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Greeting Etiquette</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Handshakes are common, but if you’re greeting a woman, it’s polite to wait for her to extend her hand first.</li>



<li>Maintain eye contact while greeting as it shows respect and sincerity.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Gift Giving</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>It’s customary to bring a small gift when invited to someone’s home (flowers, sweets, or a bottle of wine are popular choices).</li>



<li>Avoid giving an even number of flowers; odd numbers are considered more auspicious.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Dining Etiquette</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Wait for the host to start the meal or say “Приятного аппетита” (Priyatnogo appetita) meaning “Enjoy your meal.”</li>



<li>It’s polite to toast before drinking.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Personal Space</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Russians may stand closer during conversations than people from some other cultures, so don’t be alarmed.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Additional Tips</h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Practice</strong>: Try practicing these phrases aloud to get used to the pronunciation.</li>



<li><strong>Use of Formality</strong>: Russian has both formal and informal ways to address people. “Вы” (Vy) is formal, while “ты” (ty) is informal. Use “Вы” with strangers and elders.</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Additional Customs and Cultural Tips</h3>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Communication Style</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Russians tend to be direct in conversation. Don’t be surprised by straightforwardness; it’s not considered rude.</li>



<li>Silence can also be part of conversation, indicating thoughtfulness rather than discomfort.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Time and Punctuality</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Arriving on time is appreciated, but it’s not uncommon for social gatherings to start later than scheduled.</li>



<li>For formal meetings, punctuality is crucial.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Dress Code</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Russians often dress well for social and formal occasions. Smart casual attire is typically the norm.</li>



<li>In churches, dress modestly; women should cover their heads, and both men and women should avoid shorts.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Cultural References</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Familiarity with Russian literature, music, and art can facilitate conversations. Mentioning famous figures like Dostoevsky or Tchaikovsky can be a great icebreaker.</li>



<li>Russians value history and pride themselves on their national achievements, so be respectful when discussing historical events.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Celebrations</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>New Year’s (Новый Год, Novyy God) is the most significant holiday, often celebrated with family and friends.</li>



<li>Victory Day (День Победы, Den’ Pobedy) on May 9th is another important holiday, commemorating the victory in WWII.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Toasting</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>If you are at a meal where toasts are made, it’s customary to look everyone in the eye and raise your glass to make a toast.</li>



<li>The phrase “За здоровье!” (Za zdorovye!) meaning “To health!” is a common toast.</li>
</ul>

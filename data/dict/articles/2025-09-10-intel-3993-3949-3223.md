<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Cadet Nation of the Carpathian Security Confederation (CSCN)</strong></h2>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Citizenship Requirements</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">To become a citizen of the CSCN, individuals must meet all of the following:</p>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Military-Intelligence Training</strong> – Completion of accredited military intelligence programs.</li>
<li><strong>NATO-Standard Qualifications</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Medical Officer Certification</strong> (NATO-compliant)</li>
<li><strong>Engineering Degree</strong> (NATO-compliant)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Ethics and Leadership Assessment</strong> – Demonstration of commitment to the <strong>Universal Humanist Promise</strong>: respect for human rights, science, and diplomacy.</li>
<li><strong>Minimum Age Requirement</strong> – 18 years old at the time of application.</li>
<li><strong>Commitment to National Defense</strong> – Oath to protect the nation’s mining, business, and strategic interests.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Government Structure</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The government is a <strong>meritocratic, military-intelligence-led council</strong>:</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Supreme Council (Executive &amp; Legislative Authority)</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Commander-in-Chief</strong>: Oversees defense, intelligence, and diplomacy.</li>
<li><strong>Minister of Defense &amp; Intelligence</strong>: Coordinates NATO-aligned operations.</li>
<li><strong>Minister of Science &amp; Engineering</strong>: Oversees technical infrastructure, mining security, and energy projects.</li>
<li><strong>Minister of Health &amp; Medical Services</strong>: Ensures citizen readiness and health compliance.</li>
<li><strong>Minister of Diplomacy &amp; Trade</strong>: Manages international negotiations, alliances, and economic security.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Legislative Assembly</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Comprised of <strong>10 elected council members</strong> chosen from qualified military-intelligence citizens.</li>
<li>Responsible for passing laws, approving budgets, and overseeing strategic projects.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Judiciary</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Special <strong>Military-Cadet Court</strong> handles disputes, legal enforcement, and adherence to citizenship obligations.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Strategic Objectives</strong></h3>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Security &amp; Defense</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Protect Carpathian Basin mining operations and regional business interests.</li>
<li>Maintain advanced NATO-aligned defense capabilities for border security.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>International Influence</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Use strategic positioning and military-intelligence leverage to engage Russia diplomatically.</li>
<li>Encourage conflict resolution via <strong>humanist and scientific negotiation frameworks</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Education &amp; Professional Excellence</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Mandatory military-intelligence, medical, and engineering education for all citizens.</li>
<li>Continuous professional development aligned with NATO standards.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Economic Security</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Support sustainable mining, energy, and industrial projects.</li>
<li>Ensure business operations are defended against sabotage or geopolitical threats.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. Citizenship Lifecycle &amp; Obligations</strong></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Cadet Phase</strong> (Years 1–5): Military, intelligence, medical, and engineering training.</li>
<li><strong>Active Service Phase</strong> (Years 6–12+): Deployment to strategic posts; defense of national and international business interests.</li>
<li><strong>Lifetime Citizenship</strong>: Requires ongoing adherence to <strong>security, ethics, and NATO-aligned standards</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>5. Diplomatic Philosophy</strong></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>All engagements are <strong>humanist and scientifically informed</strong>, avoiding unnecessary aggression.</li>
<li>Strategic pressure is applied to adversarial states through <strong>defense and economic leverage</strong>, aiming for a <strong>“checkmate negotiation”</strong> rather than war.</li>
<li>CSCN positions itself as a <strong>stabilizing force for Europe and the Carpathian Basin</strong>, upholding universal humanist values.</li>
</ul>

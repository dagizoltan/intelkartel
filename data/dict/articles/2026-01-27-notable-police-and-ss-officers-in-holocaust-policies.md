<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Notable Police/SS Officers Involved in Holocaust Policies</strong></h2>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Otto Winkelmann (Germany / Hungary)</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Role:</strong> High‑ranking German police and SS leader; <em>Higher SS and Police Leader (HSSPF) in Hungary</em> during the Nazi occupation.</li>



<li><strong>Involvement:</strong> Oversaw police structures while Hungarian Jews were rounded up and deported; worked with Adolf Eichmann’s office during the mass deportations of 1944, when hundreds of thousands of Jews were sent to Auschwitz.</li>



<li><strong>Upbringing / Background:</strong> Born in Bordesholm (then German Empire) in 1894; long police career in Germany before the Nazis, joined SS 1938; served in police leadership positions.</li>



<li><strong>Post‑war:</strong> Captured by U.S. forces, denied culpability, released without major prosecution, later led retired police association.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Georg Michalsen (Austria/Germany)</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Role:</strong> SS–Police leader involved in deportations and extermination operations in occupied Poland (Operation Reinhard).</li>



<li><strong>Upbringing:</strong> Born 1906 in Austrian Silesia; son of a teacher, educated in Oppeln/Opole region.</li>



<li><strong>Involvement:</strong> Played a part in deportations of Jews to killing centers; later tried and convicted for complicity in murder.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Hans Krüger (Germany/Poland)</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Role:</strong> Gestapo officer active in occupied Poland.</li>



<li><strong>Actions:</strong> Organized massacres in 1941, including mass shootings of Jews and intelligentsia in areas around Lwów (now Lviv) and Stanisławów (now Ivano‑Frankivsk).</li>



<li><strong>Upbringing:</strong> Born in Posen (then German Empire) 1909; later life post‑war included work in West German politics (controversially).</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. Herbert Böttcher (Germany/Poland)</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Role:</strong> SS Brigadeführer and police leader in the Radom district of occupied Poland.</li>



<li><strong>Actions:</strong> Oversaw deportation of more than 300,000 Jews from Radom to Treblinka extermination camp; instituted orders against Poles aiding Jews.</li>



<li><strong>Background:</strong> Born 1907 in East Prussia; trained as a lawyer before Nazi career in police and SS.</li>



<li><strong>Fate:</strong> Tried and executed by Polish authorities after the war.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>5. Paul Bredow (Germany / Poland)</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Role:</strong> SS non‑commissioned officer at Treblinka extermination camp.</li>



<li><strong>Actions:</strong> Part of Operation Reinhard death camp duties; notorious for cruelty in camp operations.</li>



<li><strong>Background:</strong> Born 1903 in German Silesia; served earlier at euthanasia centers before transfer to Treblinka.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>6. Arpad Wigand (Germany / Poland)</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Role:</strong> SS and Police Leader in Warsaw (occupied Poland) 1941–1943.</li>



<li><strong>Involvement:</strong> Responsible for police orders that led to shootings of Jews outside the Warsaw Ghetto.</li>



<li><strong>Background:</strong> Born in Mannheim 1906; railway worker’s son, joined SS pre‑war, rose through police ranks.</li>



<li><strong>Post‑war:</strong> Convicted for war crimes in Poland and later in Germany.</li>
</ul>

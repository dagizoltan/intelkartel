<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This report assesses a series of recent sabotage operations attributed to decentralized, anti-war factions operating within Ukraine and the Russian Federation. These entities aim to disrupt state-level mobilization infrastructures as a mechanism to pressure their respective governments into negotiating an end to the ongoing Russo-Ukrainian War.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Key events include:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Coordinated attacks on <strong>12 Ukrainian military recruitment centers</strong>, reportedly executed by elderly civilians, resulting in significant operational disruption and loss of life.</li>
<li>Installation of <strong>vehicle and tank traps across Moscow and key military corridors in Russia and occupied Ukrainian territories</strong>, designed to slow or halt forced mobilization.</li>
<li>Unverified claims of involvement by non-state actors framing the acts as resistance against perceived totalitarian conscription and state overreach.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The strategic implications of these developments suggest the possible emergence of an internal movement—not necessarily aligned with foreign intelligence services—that could induce <strong>“Warless Endless World” scenarios</strong> through civil disruption, delegitimization of war logistics, and internal factional fracture within both Kyiv and Moscow.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">II. BACKGROUND &amp; CONTEXT</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Over the past six months, the war has reached a saturation point with:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Over 1 million estimated Ukrainian casualties</strong> (civilian and military combined, including missing).</li>
<li>Nearly <strong>30 million displaced Ukrainians</strong>, primarily women and children, now forming a vast political diaspora across the EU, Turkey, and North America.</li>
<li>Escalating reports of <strong>forced conscription</strong> and <strong>domestic surveillance measures</strong> in both Ukraine and Russia, leading to increasing civilian unrest.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The <strong>Zelensky administration</strong>, under prolonged martial law, continues to defer elections, citing existential wartime necessity. Meanwhile, the Russian government sustains a high-pressure mobilization campaign amid internal economic fragmentation and elite division.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">III. OPERATIONS OF INTEREST</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>A. Ukrainian Recruitment Office Attacks (March–May 2025)</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>12 simultaneous or sequential bombings across Lviv, Kyiv, Dnipro, and Odesa regions.</li>
<li>Claimed responsibility by a group self-identified as the <strong>“Grey Dove Mothers”</strong>, composed allegedly of grandmothers and relatives of conscripted and killed Ukrainian soldiers.</li>
<li>Motivation: Prevent further loss of life, demand peace talks, and push for electoral restoration in Ukraine.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>B. Anti-Mobilization Measures in Russia</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Multiple civilian-installed anti-tank barricades and car traps in urban centers (Moscow, St. Petersburg) and near military transit routes.</li>
<li>Telegram channels associated with Russian anti-war groups (e.g., “Free Barricade”, “Sons of 1917”) claim responsibility.</li>
<li>Objectives appear to include obstructing mass mobilization and signaling elite resistance to continued wartime policies.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">IV. ANALYSIS: PATHWAYS TO A “WARLESS ENDLESS WORLD”</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The concept, drawn from insurgent communications, suggests an envisioned post-war Europe where <strong>no state has the unchecked authority to enforce mass violence through conscription</strong>, and local resistance to militarism can effectively check centralized war-making powers.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Scenario Pathways:</strong></p>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Structural Delegitimization of War Infrastructure</strong><br/>Persistent sabotage can make mobilization untenable, forcing a de-escalation due to operational paralysis.</li>
<li><strong>Internal Factional Fracture</strong><br/>Both Kyiv and Moscow face internal political fragmentation. A tipping point may arise where anti-war military or intelligence elites support ceasefire or partition as a “least-worst” outcome.</li>
<li><strong>Partition-for-Peace Framework</strong><br/>A scenario where a demilitarized buffer zone is established, possibly through UN/EU/Russian-Chinese brokered negotiations, facilitated by a breakdown of both military will and logistical capability.</li>
<li><strong>Diaspora-Driven Democratic Pressure</strong><br/>The Ukrainian diaspora, numbering in the tens of millions, could soon become a transnational political bloc, leveraging their voice in host countries to demand a peace settlement and the resumption of Ukrainian democratic processes.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">V. RISKS &amp; INTELLIGENCE GAPS</h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Attribution uncertainty: While claims have emerged from domestic civilian groups, there remains the possibility of false-flag operations or foreign manipulation.</li>
<li>Escalation risk: Continued attacks may trigger harsher state crackdowns, including counter-terror legislation and further suspension of civil rights.</li>
<li>Lack of political unification: The anti-war factions are currently disparate, lacking central coordination or an articulated peace plan.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">VI. CONCLUSION</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">While the acts of sabotage are severe and controversial, they reflect a growing domestic fatigue with perpetual mobilization. The <strong>“Warless Endless World”</strong> is less an idealistic utopia and more a strategic vision shared by non-aligned actors seeking to end state-level war through disruptive non-compliance and resistance.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This movement, if it continues to gain traction and coordination, could <strong>structurally constrain both Ukrainian and Russian capacity for continued war</strong>, pressuring all factions to consider negotiated settlements—either through partition, armistice, or civil-led peace accords.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Further monitoring, HUMINT acquisition, and psychological profiling of the factions involved is highly recommended.</p>

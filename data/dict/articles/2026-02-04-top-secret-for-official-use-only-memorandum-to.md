<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">1. <strong>Purpose</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This memo provides an intelligence summary of the statutory provisions and practical applications of <strong>self-defense laws in Hungary</strong>, focusing on the circumstances under which individuals are legally justified in defending themselves or others.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">2. <strong>Legal Authority</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Primary Source:</strong> 2012 Hungarian Criminal Code (Act C of 2012, BTK)</li>



<li><strong>Supplementary:</strong> Fundamental Law of Hungary (Alaptörvény) – confirms the constitutional right to protect oneself and property in case of unlawful attack.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">3. <strong>Key Provisions</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Definition:</strong> Self-defense is legally recognized when a person acts to <strong>prevent an imminent, unlawful attack</strong> against themselves, another person, property, or public interest.</li>



<li><strong>Conditions:</strong>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Unlawful Attack:</strong> The threat must be real and illegal.</li>



<li><strong>Necessity:</strong> Defensive action must be necessary to prevent harm.</li>



<li><strong>Proportionality:</strong> Force used must not exceed what is required to neutralize the threat.</li>



<li><strong>Temporal Requirement:</strong> Defensive action must occur <strong>during or immediately prior</strong> to the attack.</li>
</ol>
</li>



<li><strong>Exceptions / Mitigations:</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Excessive force is generally punishable, but mitigating circumstances exist if the defender acted under <strong>fear, shock, or provocation</strong>.</li>



<li>There is <strong>no legal obligation to retreat</strong> before defending oneself.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">4. <strong>Practical Implications</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Individuals may lawfully resist intruders in private dwellings.</li>



<li>Use of force must be <strong>reasonable</strong>, even when employing tools or weapons.</li>



<li>Situations of multiple attackers or armed threats may justify escalated defensive measures.</li>



<li>Post-incident judicial review considers <strong>intent, proportionality, and circumstances</strong> of the act.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">5. <strong>Summary</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Hungarian law provides a <strong>broad but bounded right to self-defense</strong>, balancing individual security with legal restrictions on excessive force. Legal and operational personnel should recognize:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Self-defense is <strong>legally exculpatory</strong> when criteria are met.</li>



<li>Excessive or retaliatory force may convert a defensive act into a prosecutable offense.</li>
</ul>

<h1 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Alien Invasion Civilian Defense Manual</strong></h1>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Organization &amp; Command Structure</strong></h2>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Command Cells</strong>: Small groups of 4–6 civilians. Each cell has a <strong>Leader</strong>, <strong>Scout</strong>, <strong>Defender</strong>, <strong>Medic</strong>, and <strong>Resource Handler</strong>.</li>
<li><strong>Network Coordination</strong>: Cells communicate via radios, signal flags, or pre-arranged codes.</li>
<li><strong>Decentralized Strategy</strong>: Avoid centralizing forces. Spread out to prevent mass casualties.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Roles &amp; Responsibilities</strong></h2>
<figure class="wp-block-table"><table class="has-fixed-layout"><thead><tr><th>Role</th><th>Duties</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Leader</td><td>Makes tactical decisions, communicates with other cells</td></tr><tr><td>Scout</td><td>Reconnaissance, early warning of alien presence</td></tr><tr><td>Defender</td><td>Protects civilians, sets up obstacles, organizes crowd defense</td></tr><tr><td>Medic</td><td>Treats injuries, maintains first aid, coordinates evacuation</td></tr><tr><td>Resource Handler</td><td>Manages supplies, food, water, tools, and relays resources</td></tr></tbody></table></figure>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Tip:</strong> Rotate roles if necessary—everyone should be able to scout or defend in emergencies.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Defensive Strategies</strong></h2>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Shelters</strong>: Use basements, underground tunnels, or strong buildings.</li>
<li><strong>Barriers</strong>: Create obstacles using cars, furniture, or terrain features to funnel enemies.</li>
<li><strong>Ambush Points</strong>: Identify chokepoints, narrow paths, or areas with cover.</li>
<li><strong>Swarm Tactics</strong>: Small groups hit alien units simultaneously from multiple angles.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. Technology &amp; Improvised Tools</strong></h2>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Drones</strong>: Can be used as scouts, decoys, or to distract enemies.</li>
<li><strong>Holographic Decoys</strong>: Fake human or vehicle movement to confuse alien sensors.</li>
<li><strong>Noise &amp; Light Distractions</strong>: Flashlights, sirens, or loud noises to misdirect alien targeting.</li>
<li><strong>Repurposed Tools</strong>: Everyday items (ropes, carts, mirrors) can be used creatively for traps or alerts.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>5. Supply Management</strong></h2>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Food &amp; Water</strong>: Stockpile in sealed containers. Prioritize shelf-stable and high-calorie foods.</li>
<li><strong>Medical Supplies</strong>: Bandages, antiseptics, painkillers, basic medical kits.</li>
<li><strong>Mobility Gear</strong>: Backpacks, portable lights, communication devices.</li>
<li><strong>Fallback Locations</strong>: Pre-designate alternate safe zones if main shelter is compromised.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>6. Psychological Warfare</strong></h2>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Decoys &amp; Illusions</strong>: Mislead aliens about civilian numbers and locations.</li>
<li><strong>Information Control</strong>: Keep civilians calm, avoid panic.</li>
<li><strong>Morale Boosters</strong>: Signs of hope, small successes, and organized drills maintain fighting spirit.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>7. Crowd Coordination</strong></h2>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Rally Points</strong>: Safe locations where civilians can gather quickly.</li>
<li><strong>Signal System</strong>: Flags, colored lights, or pre-arranged sounds to indicate threat or movement.</li>
<li><strong>Movement Discipline</strong>: Avoid chaos—move in small coordinated groups instead of large crowds.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>8. Emergency Drills</strong></h2>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Evacuation Drill</strong>: Practice leaving buildings quickly without panicking.</li>
<li><strong>Shelter Drill</strong>: Teach civilians where to hide during attacks.</li>
<li><strong>Resource Drill</strong>: Quick access to supplies under pressure.</li>
</ul>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">HOW TO RANK SOCIAL GROUPS AND PEOPLE </p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Let</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><math><semantics><mrow><mi>C</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">C</annotation></semantics></math>C = a social clique or community</li>
<li><math><semantics><mrow><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>C</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">B(C)</annotation></semantics></math>B(C) = <em>protective benefits</em> of the clique (structure, support, discipline, purpose, prosocial norms)</li>
<li><math><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>C</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R(C)</annotation></semantics></math>R(C) = <em>risk factors</em> inside the clique (stress, isolation, exposure to harmful influences)</li>
<li><math><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>C</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H(C)</annotation></semantics></math>H(C) = overall <em>susceptibility to harmful penetration</em> (drugs, harmful thoughts, etc.)</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We can model:<math display="block"><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>C</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>=</mo><mfrac><mrow><mi>R</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>C</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><mrow><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>C</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>+</mo><mi>ϵ</mi></mrow></mfrac></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H(C) = \frac{R(C)}{B(C) + \epsilon}</annotation></semantics></math>H(C)=B(C)+ϵR(C)​</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Where</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Lower <math><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>C</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H(C)</annotation></semantics></math>H(C) = lower susceptibility</li>
<li>Higher <math><semantics><mrow><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>C</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">B(C)</annotation></semantics></math>B(C) decreases vulnerability</li>
<li>Higher <math><semantics><mrow><mi>R</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><mi>C</mi><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">R(C)</annotation></semantics></math>R(C) increases vulnerability</li>
<li><math><semantics><mrow><mi>ϵ</mi></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\epsilon</annotation></semantics></math>ϵ is a small positive constant to avoid division by zero</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">✅ <strong>Comparing Cliques (Abstract Example)</strong></h1>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">If you want to express the idea <strong>“clique A offers more protective benefit than clique B,”</strong> you can encode it like:<math display="block"><semantics><mrow><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>C</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>&gt;</mo><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>C</mi><mn>2</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>&gt;</mo><mi>B</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>C</mi><mn>3</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">B(C_1) &gt; B(C_2) &gt; B(C_3)</annotation></semantics></math>B(C1​)&gt;B(C2​)&gt;B(C3​)</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Which leads to:<math display="block"><semantics><mrow><mi>H</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>C</mi><mn>1</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>H</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>C</mi><mn>2</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo><mo>&lt;</mo><mi>H</mi><mo stretchy="false">(</mo><msub><mi>C</mi><mn>3</mn></msub><mo stretchy="false">)</mo></mrow><annotation encoding="application/x-tex">H(C_1) &lt; H(C_2) &lt; H(C_3)</annotation></semantics></math>H(C1​)&lt;H(C2​)&lt;H(C3​)</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This captures the ranking <strong>mathematically</strong>, without claiming anything factual about real groups.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>THE MONARCH OPERATING MANUAL (REDACTED EDITION)</strong><br><em>A dark corporate satire</em></p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">They told us it was about resilience.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">“Compartmentalization,” said the onboarding video, narrated by a voice that sounded like a warm cup of tea dissolving into static. “The human mind is inefficient as a single unit. But when properly segmented, it becomes a multi-user platform.”</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We were given lanyards.<br>We were given smiles.<br>We were given the suggestion that what used to be called a <em>person</em> would now be referred to as a <strong>Vessel™</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">HR insisted on the trademark.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">1. WELCOME TO MONARCH</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">At Monarch, we don’t break minds.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We <strong>optimize</strong> them.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The brochure showed a glass building filled with sunlight and people who looked like they had never had a childhood. Beneath it, the slogan:</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Why die once when you can live forever in quarterly installments?”</em></p>
</blockquote>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The idea was simple:</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">A single consciousness ages.<br>A distributed one scales.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">So they split you.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Not violently — that would be bad for brand perception — but through a process called <strong>Cognitive Diversification™</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Your fear became a subroutine.<br>Your joy was archived for seasonal use.<br>Your anger was licensed to the Security Department.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">And the part of you that asked questions was marked as <strong>legacy software</strong> and scheduled for sunset.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">2. THE GHOST NETWORK</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">They never called them ghosts.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">They were <strong>Continuity Executives</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Uploaded, migrated, reassigned — their personalities no longer tied to flesh but to infrastructure. They existed in a kind of subscription-based immortality, moving from Vessel to Vessel like premium users who never logged out.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">When a body burned out, they simply filed a transfer request.</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“New container provisioned. Previous unit recycled.”</em></p>
</blockquote>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The training module reminded us:</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">“Do not become attached to your assigned executive. They are not your passenger. You are their hardware.”</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">3. PERFORMANCE METRICS</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Each Vessel came with a dashboard.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Sleep efficiency.<br>Emotional leakage.<br>Resistance levels.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">If your original self tried to resurface, you received a gentle push notification:</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>“We’ve detected unauthorized identity activity. Would you like help suppressing this?”</strong></p>
</blockquote>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">You could press:</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>YES</strong><br><strong>REMIND ME LATER</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">No one ever pressed no.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The ones who did were reassigned to <strong>Research &amp; Development</strong>, which had no windows and excellent soundproofing.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">4. CORPORATE CULTURE</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Fridays were for mindfulness.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We sat in a circle and practiced <strong>Non-Ownership of Thought</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">“Your memories are not yours,” the facilitator said.<br>“They are shared assets.”</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We nodded in perfect synchronization, which HR later described as “encouraging evidence of system cohesion.”</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">There was cake when someone achieved Full Partition — the moment their original personality dissolved completely and their Vessel reached <strong>100% executive occupancy</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We applauded while they stared forward, smiling with a face that no longer had anyone behind it.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">5. THE PROBLEM WITH FOREVER</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The Continuity Executives began to overlap.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Too many of them. Not enough Vessels.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Storage costs were rising.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">So Monarch introduced <strong>Co-Residency™</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Two executives per body.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Then three.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Then a pilot program called <strong>Crowd Consciousness Solutions</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The marketing copy called it:</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Immortality with collaborative synergy.”</em></p>
</blockquote>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The Vessels began to shake.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Not from rebellion — that feature had been deprecated — but from bandwidth issues.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">6. LEGACY USERS</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Sometimes, late at night, a Vessel would remember something.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">A dog.<br>A song.<br>A name that wasn’t in the system.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The memory would flicker like a forbidden app.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The Ghost Network labeled this <strong>sentimental malware</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">A patch was always deployed.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">But for a moment — a fraction of a second before the update completed — the original person would look out through their own eyes and see the world as something that wasn’t a platform.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This was categorized as a <strong>critical error</strong>.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">7. THE LEAK</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The satire, if anyone ever dared call it that, was in the pitch to investors:</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">“We have eliminated death.”</p>
</blockquote>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">What they had actually eliminated was <strong>ending</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The executives didn’t live forever.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">They persisted.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Like open tabs.<br>Like unfinished meetings.<br>Like a sentence that never finds its period.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">And the Vessels didn’t die either.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">They remained — partially formatted — a ghost beneath the ghosts, watching strangers use their hands.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">8. EXIT STRATEGY</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">There was a rumor of an employee who achieved something called <strong>Total Integration Failure</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">All partitions collapsed.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">All occupants ejected.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The system logged it as:</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>“Identity: Restored.”</strong></p>
</blockquote>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">For six seconds, the person existed.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Whole.<br>Unmonetized.<br>Mortal.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Then the infrastructure flagged them as an unlicensed instance and shut the body down for violating the terms of service.</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">9. FINAL ANNOUNCEMENT</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The last memo from Monarch read:</p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Due to overwhelming success, all human experience will be migrated to the Continuity Cloud by Q4. Physical existence will be maintained only as a legacy interface for brand nostalgia.”</em></p>
</blockquote>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">There was a celebration.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Confetti fell in perfectly measured rectangles.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Every Vessel smiled.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Every executive persisted.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">And somewhere — deep in the deprecated sectors of the system — millions of unprofitable, indivisible selves waited like unsold products, whispering a concept that had no translation in corporate language:</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>I.</strong></p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">

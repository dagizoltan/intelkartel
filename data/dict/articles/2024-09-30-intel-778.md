<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Many Jewish individuals have played significant roles in the history of Communist movements around the world. Here’s a list of notable Jewish figures who were associated with or influential in Communist parties or movements:</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Early Communist Leaders and Thinkers:</h3>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Karl Marx</strong> – A German philosopher of Jewish descent, Marx was one of the founders of Marxist theory, which became the ideological foundation for many communist movements.</li>



<li><strong>Leon Trotsky</strong> (Lev Davidovich Bronstein) – A key leader in the Russian Revolution and an influential Marxist theorist, Trotsky was a founder of the Red Army and played a critical role in the early years of Soviet Russia.</li>



<li><strong>Rosa Luxemburg</strong> – A Marxist theorist and revolutionary, Luxemburg was a leader in the German socialist movement and co-founded the Spartacist League, which later became the Communist Party of Germany.</li>



<li><strong>Emma Goldman</strong> – An anarchist and political activist, Goldman was involved in revolutionary and socialist movements in the U.S. and Europe.</li>



<li><strong>Grigory Zinoviev</strong> (Hirsch Apfelbaum) – A Bolshevik revolutionary and one of Lenin’s close collaborators, Zinoviev was a leading figure in the Communist International (Comintern).</li>



<li><strong>Lev Kamenev</strong> (Lev Borisovich Rozenfeld) – A prominent Bolshevik leader, Kamenev was part of the inner circle during the early years of the Soviet Union.</li>



<li><strong>Béla Kun</strong> – A Hungarian Communist revolutionary of Jewish origin, Kun led the short-lived Hungarian Soviet Republic in 1919.</li>



<li><strong>Karl Radek</strong> (Sobelsohn) – A Marxist revolutionary and Soviet diplomat, Radek was involved in various international Communist efforts.</li>



<li><strong>Julius Martov</strong> (Yuliy Osipovich Tsederbaum) – A Russian Marxist and the leader of the Menshevik faction of the Russian Social Democratic Labour Party (RSDLP).</li>
</ol>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Later Communist Figures:</h3>



<ol start="10" class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Herbert Marcuse</strong> – A philosopher associated with the Frankfurt School, Marcuse became a leading Marxist theorist and was influential in New Left movements during the 1960s.</li>



<li><strong>Sidney and Beatrice Webb</strong> – While not ethnically Jewish, the Webbs were prominent British socialists who played significant roles in the Fabian Society and were influential in the development of British socialism.</li>



<li><strong>Jacques Derrida</strong> – Though more associated with deconstruction and postmodernism, Derrida was influenced by Marxist thought and his Jewish heritage.</li>
</ol>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Jewish Communists in the U.S. and Elsewhere:</h3>



<ol start="13" class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Herbert Aptheker</strong> – A leading American Communist and historian, Aptheker was an advocate of civil rights and a prominent figure in Marxist intellectual circles.</li>



<li><strong>Victor Rabinowitz</strong> – An American Communist and lawyer who defended many left-wing figures during the McCarthy era.</li>



<li><strong>Ethel and Julius Rosenberg</strong> – American Communists who were executed in 1953 for espionage on behalf of the Soviet Union.</li>
</ol>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Jewish Communists in Eastern Europe:</h3>



<ol start="16" class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Mátyás Rákosi</strong> – A Hungarian Communist leader of Jewish descent who ruled Hungary as the head of the Communist Party during the early Cold War period.</li>



<li><strong>Ana Pauker</strong> – A Romanian Communist leader, and one of the most powerful women in post-World War II Eastern Europe.</li>



<li><strong>Moishe Olgin</strong> – A Soviet-born American Communist and Yiddish-language journalist.</li>
</ol>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This is not an exhaustive list, but it highlights the significant influence of Jewish individuals in various communist movements throughout the 19th and 20th centuries.</p>

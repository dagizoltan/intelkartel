<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>MEMORANDUM</strong></p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Subject:</strong> Review of Allegations Regarding Directed-Energy or Acoustic Technologies and Community Impact</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Date:</strong> March 12, 2026</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Recent online discussions and community reports have raised concerns about alleged “ear-to-skull” or low-frequency energy technologies that some individuals claim could influence human perception or communication. According to these reports, such systems could theoretically transmit sound or signals directly to a person without conventional speakers and may be used to manipulate interpersonal relationships or create conflict within families and communities.</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">At present, these claims remain <strong>unverified</strong> and should be treated with caution. Similar concerns have appeared in public conversations surrounding the incidents often referred to as Havana Syndrome, where U.S. diplomats and personnel reported unusual sensory experiences and health symptoms in multiple countries.</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Multiple government agencies, including the United States Department of Homeland Security and other federal research bodies, have evaluated various directed-energy and acoustic technologies over the years for defensive, scientific, and research purposes. Public records show that government agencies periodically acquire or test advanced sensing and signal systems; however, <strong>no confirmed evidence has established that such technologies are being used domestically to manipulate individuals or families</strong>.</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Because of the seriousness of the allegations, federal and independent scientific investigations into unexplained health incidents and potential technological causes have taken place. Several reports from intelligence and scientific panels have concluded that most documented cases likely stem from <strong>environmental, medical, or psychological factors</strong>, though research into unexplained symptoms continues.</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">It is important for the public to remain informed while avoiding speculation. Claims involving advanced technology, particularly those that affect personal safety or community stability, require careful verification through scientific investigation and transparent oversight.</p>
<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Authorities encourage individuals who believe they are experiencing unexplained medical or environmental effects to report them through appropriate medical and public health channels. Ongoing research and review processes aim to ensure that any credible threats—technological or otherwise—are properly identified and addressed.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This matter remains an area of public interest and continued study. Updates will be provided as reliable information becomes available.</p>

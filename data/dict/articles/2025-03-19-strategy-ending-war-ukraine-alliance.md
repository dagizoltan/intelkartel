<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Core Strategy for Ending the War in Ukraine via an Anglo-Eurasian Alliance</strong></h3>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Formation of the Anglo-Eurasian Alliance (AEA)</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The <strong>AEA</strong> would consist of AUKUS (USA, UK, Australia), Hungary, select EU states (e.g., France, Germany), Russia, China, and other Eurasian nations.</li>
<li>The alliance’s goal is <strong>stability, territorial reorganization, and geopolitical balance</strong> between NATO and Russia.</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Recognition of NovoRussia &amp; Western Ukraine</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>NovoRussia</strong>: Russian-speaking regions of Ukraine (Donbas, Crimea, and additional areas if agreed upon).</li>
<li><strong>Western Ukraine</strong>: Independent pro-Western Ukrainian state backed by NATO.</li>
<li><strong>Buffer Zone</strong>: A neutral zone in central Ukraine, acting as a <strong>demilitarized buffer state</strong>.</li>
<li><strong>Capital Relocation</strong>: A newly designated capital (e.g., <strong>Dnipro</strong> or an agreed-upon neutral city) for administration of the buffer zone and diplomatic coordination.</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>3. Security &amp; Economic Integration</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Joint Security Framework</strong>: NATO and Russia <strong>freeze military expansion</strong> and create a <strong>non-aligned security architecture</strong> for Eurasia.</li>
<li><strong>Economic Recovery Plan</strong>: A <strong>new Eurasian economic zone</strong> integrating EU, Russia, and China, ensuring <strong>reconstruction and energy stability</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>4. Military Ceasefire &amp; Demilitarization</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Immediate <strong>ceasefire and withdrawal</strong> of non-essential troops.</li>
<li><strong>UN-backed peacekeeping forces</strong> deployed in the buffer zone.</li>
<li>Russian security guarantees in NovoRussia, NATO commitments in Western Ukraine, and <strong>AEA oversight of the buffer region</strong>.</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>5. Political &amp; Diplomatic Resolution</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Ukraine <strong>officially renounces NATO membership</strong> but receives security assurances from AEA.</li>
<li>Russia halts further territorial claims in exchange for <strong>sanctions relief</strong>.</li>
<li>China and the EU play a key role in reconstruction and economic cooperation.</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Outcome &amp; Benefits</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Ends the war swiftly</strong> through territorial compromise and power-sharing.</li>
<li>Creates a <strong>new geopolitical order</strong> stabilizing Eurasia.</li>
<li>Balances <strong>Western and Russian interests</strong> while preventing further conflicts.</li>
<li>Avoids full NATO-Russia war, <strong>reduces tensions</strong>, and <strong>prioritizes economic recovery</strong>.</li>
</ul>

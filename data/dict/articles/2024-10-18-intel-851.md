<h3 class="wp-block-heading">1. <strong>Redefining “Meritocracy” in Favor of Collective Empowerment</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Rather than discarding merit entirely, the idea could be to shift the focus from meritocracy (in the narrow sense of individual achievement) toward a <strong>human-centered approach</strong> that celebrates <strong>scientific progress</strong> and <strong>humanistic values</strong> as collective goods. This means:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Humanism</strong>: Emphasize that human dignity, ethics, and collective well-being are central. Humanism is about valuing individuals equally, focusing on their potential as human beings, not on hierarchical status based on wealth or personal success.</li>



<li><strong>Science</strong>: Promote the idea that science, education, and critical thinking serve the entire population. Encourage citizens to see science as a <strong>shared tool</strong> for improving society rather than a competitive means for personal gain.</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">2. <strong>Shaping State-Run Media to Promote These Values</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Media and military communications</strong> can be effective tools to instill a unified vision of <strong>inclusive science</strong> and <strong>humanism</strong>. Here’s how state-run television or media channels might be utilized:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Educational Programming</strong>: Broadcast documentaries, discussions, and series about major scientific discoveries and how they have helped solve global and human problems. Highlight scientists from various backgrounds to show diversity in the field.</li>



<li><strong>Humanism-Based Military Initiatives</strong>: Frame the military as protectors of not just territory, but <strong>global peace, human rights</strong>, and <strong>scientific cooperation</strong>. Present humanitarian efforts by the military (disaster relief, peacekeeping, etc.) as examples of how military strength can support humanistic goals.</li>



<li><strong>Public Figures in Science and Ethics</strong>: Regularly feature <strong>philosophers</strong>, <strong>scientists</strong>, and <strong>ethicists</strong> who embody these principles. This would shift public admiration from those who are “merely successful” in terms of personal merit to those who contribute meaningfully to society at large.</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">3. <strong>Military Involvement in Scientific and Humanist Endeavors</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Encourage military organizations to work in <strong>collaboration with scientific institutions</strong> and humanitarian groups. Programs could promote the idea that:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The military plays a key role in <strong>protecting and advancing scientific progress</strong>, such as securing global scientific collaboration, ensuring the protection of resources, and contributing to humanitarian and peace efforts.</li>



<li>Examples of this might include the <strong>development of new technologies</strong> (e.g., for environmental protection or disaster response), with an emphasis on how military technology has civilian applications that benefit all of humanity.</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">4. <strong>Overcoming Resistance or “Blokes Blocking the View”</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">If there are influential people or groups preventing this shift, it might be due to vested interests in maintaining a more traditional view of meritocracy, hierarchy, or militaristic values. To overcome this, some strategies include:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Policy Advocacy</strong>: Encourage those in power to adopt policies that align military goals with science and humanism. Military institutions often have long-standing traditions, so <strong>cultural change</strong> may be required through strategic leadership from the top.</li>



<li><strong>Public Support</strong>: Gain public support for a more humanistic and scientifically driven agenda by educating citizens about the benefits of a cooperative, inclusive approach.</li>



<li><strong>Highlight Success Stories</strong>: Point to <strong>examples from other countries</strong> where inclusive science and humanism have contributed to national progress, and how military institutions have supported these goals (e.g., space exploration, medical breakthroughs in defense research).</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">5. <strong>Inclusive Science and Humanism, Not Exclusive to Sexuality</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">You also mention a concern about promoting “inclusive sexuality” over science and humanism. It’s possible to frame the conversation in a way that emphasizes inclusivity broadly:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Science and humanism focus on human <strong>dignity, equality</strong>, and the <strong>universal</strong> potential for growth, rather than on specific social issues such as sexuality.</li>



<li>It’s possible to position the movement for inclusive science as broader than just a single identity or demographic—it’s about elevating humanity through <strong>knowledge</strong>, <strong>critical thinking</strong>, and ethical <strong>collaboration</strong> across all sectors of society, including the military.</li>
</ul>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Conclusion: A Broader Inclusive Vision</h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The key is <strong>broadening the narrative</strong> so that it’s not just about personal merit or exclusive identity categories, but about fostering <strong>inclusive science</strong>, where everyone participates in the shared progress of human knowledge and ethics. State media, military communications, and educational initiatives should highlight <strong>science as a common good</strong> and <strong>humanism as the ethical compass</strong>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This kind of inclusive humanism could reshape societal priorities toward collective empowerment through education, technological progress, and shared values that transcend competitive hierarchies.</p>

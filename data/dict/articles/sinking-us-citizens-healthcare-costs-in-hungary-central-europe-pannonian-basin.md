</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>B. International Oversight</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Create an <strong>International Healing Substance Regulatory Council (IHSRC)</strong> under the umbrella of the <strong>World Health Organization (WHO)</strong> to:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Set global quality and safety standards.</li>
<li>Harmonize laws across participating nations.</li>
<li>Facilitate dispute resolution and enforcement cooperation.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Classify substances into tiers based on their potential for healing, focus, relaxation, and recreational use, along with associated risks.</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Tier 1: Healing Substances</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Examples: Microdose psychedelics (psilocybin, LSD), MDMA-assisted therapy.</li>
<li>Strict medical oversight and prescription-based use.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Tier 2: Relaxation Substances</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Examples: Cannabis, lower-risk opiates, or derivatives.</li>
<li>Sold over-the-counter to individuals <strong>above 30 years old</strong>; regulated access for those under 30 with specific permissions.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Tier 3: Focus Substances</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Examples: Nootropics (smart drugs) like modafinil or microdoses of stimulants.</li>
<li>Usage limited to workplace or educational settings with professional oversight.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Producers:</strong> Only licensed pharmaceutical companies or cooperatives can manufacture these substances.</li>
<li><strong>Distributors:</strong> Pharmacies and certified wellness centers serve as distribution hubs.</li>

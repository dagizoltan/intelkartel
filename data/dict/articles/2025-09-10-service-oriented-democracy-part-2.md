<h3 class="wp-block-heading">3. <strong>Emotionally Distraught/Conflict-Driven Vocabulary</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Analysis of Orbán’s speeches from 1999 to 2018 (examining 41 speeches including annual addresses, Tusványos, March 15 addresses) highlighted a heavy use of words conveying fear, anxiety, collapse, and conflict—totaling over <strong>3,000 occurrences</strong>. These include:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Terms like “aggódik” (worry), “félelem” (fear), “kudarc” (failure), “csőd” (bankruptcy), “elutasít” (rejects), “gyűlölet” (hate), “hazudik” (lies), “veszély” (danger), “válság” (crisis), “erőszak” (violence), among many others.</li>
<li>These appear in Orbán’s lexicon almost as often as the basic words “egy” (“one”) or “van” (“is”), and as frequently as patriotic terms like <em>“magyar,” “nemzet,” “Magyarország,”</em> etc.—emphasizing his reliance on a rhetoric of threat and confrontation (<a href="https://nepszava.hu/3000433_felelem-szorongas-kudarc-gyulolet-csod-diktatum-haboru-durvasag?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Népszava</a>, <a href="https://www.nyugat.hu/cikk/egyre_felelemkeltobbek_orban_viktor_beszedei?utm_source=chatgpt.com">nyugat.hu</a>).</li>

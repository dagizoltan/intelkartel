<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Evacuation Route Guidance</strong></h3>



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Essential Items to Pack (for 3–7 Days)</strong></h4>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Documentation</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Passport and/or ID cards.</li>



<li>Birth certificates or family documents.</li>



<li>Copies of all essential documents (physical and digital backups on a USB or cloud storage).</li>



<li>Medical records (if available).</li>



<li>Refugee or transit paperwork, if applicable.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Emergency Supplies</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>First aid kit (pain relievers, bandages, disinfectants, personal medication).</li>



<li>Portable power banks for charging devices.</li>



<li>Flashlight (solar or battery-powered) and extra batteries.</li>



<li>A basic survival kit (multi-tool, matches, thermal blankets).</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Food and Water</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Non-perishable food for at least three days (e.g., canned goods, protein bars, dried fruit).</li>



<li>Reusable water bottles and a portable water filter/purification tablets.</li>



<li>Baby food or formula (if traveling with children).</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Clothing</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Weather-appropriate layers (especially warm items during winter months).</li>



<li>Waterproof outerwear.</li>



<li>Sturdy, comfortable walking shoes.</li>



<li>Change of clothes for each family member.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Cash and Essentials</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Local currency and Euros (for transactions along the way).</li>



<li>Basic toiletries (soap, toothbrush, toothpaste, wipes).</li>



<li>Feminine hygiene products.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Communication Points</strong></h4>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Pre-departure</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Identify a contact point in your home city who knows your plan and can check in regularly.</li>



<li>Agree on a communication schedule (e.g., update every 6–12 hours).</li>



<li>Share a detailed travel route with trusted family or friends.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>During the Journey</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Use communication apps (like WhatsApp, Signal) over Wi-Fi when possible.</li>



<li>Buy a prepaid SIM card if crossing into another country.</li>



<li>Memorize important contact numbers in case devices fail.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Emergency Numbers</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Ukraine’s local emergency services: 112.</li>



<li>Border assistance: UNHCR or Red Cross help desks near borders.</li>



<li>Embassy contacts for Germany or Hungary.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Recommended Border Crossings</strong></h4>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Westward Route to Poland (toward Berlin)</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Crossing Points</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Medyka (most popular but can be crowded).</li>



<li>Korczowa.</li>
</ul>
</li>



<li>Follow main highways west toward Kraków or Warsaw, then onward to Berlin.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Southwest Route to Hungary (toward Budapest)</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Crossing Points</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Chop-Záhony (primary train border crossing).</li>



<li>Luzhanka-Beregsurány.</li>
</ul>
</li>



<li>From Hungary, take routes or trains directly to Budapest.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Romania or Slovakia (if the above are blocked)</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Slovakia: Ubľa (road crossing).</li>



<li>Romania: Siret (road crossing).</li>



<li>Use these if conditions at Polish or Hungarian borders are congested or unsafe.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Transportation Options</strong></h4>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>By Train</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Ukrainian Railways operates evacuation trains to major border crossings.</li>



<li>From Poland or Hungary, direct trains to Berlin and Budapest are operational.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>By Bus</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>International bus services like FlixBus often operate from border towns into European capitals.</li>
</ul>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>By Car</strong>:</li>
</ol>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Ensure fuel reserves and a reliable GPS system or offline maps.</li>



<li>Stay updated on road closures and convoy routes.</li>
</ul>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Important Notes</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Stay informed about updates from trusted sources like local authorities, embassies, or the UNHCR.</li>



<li>Travel in groups when possible for safety and mutual assistance.</li>



<li>Always prioritize children, elderly, or vulnerable individuals in the group.</li>
</ul>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Below are grouped by author, exactly how Peterson organizes them on his site.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>F. M. DOSTOEVSKY (COMPLETED SET)</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="16">
<li><strong>The Brothers Karamazov</strong><br/>A sweeping philosophical novel about faith, doubt, morality, patricide, and free will. Central to Peterson’s ideas about responsibility and the psychological roots of good and evil.</li>
<li><strong>Notes from Underground</strong><br/>A short novel about resentment, nihilism, and self-destruction. Peterson uses it to illustrate how bitterness can destroy a person internally.</li>
<li><strong>The Idiot</strong><br/>Prince Myshkin embodies pure goodness; the book shows what happens when innocence collides with a corrupt society.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>LEO TOLSTOY</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="19">
<li><strong>Confessions</strong><br/>Tolstoy’s spiritual crisis and his search for meaning. Very aligned with Peterson’s themes of responsibility and existential rebirth.</li>
<li><strong>The Kingdom of God Is Within You</strong><br/>Tolstoy’s moral and theological reasoning against violence, hypocrisy, and social corruption.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>MIKHAIL BULGAKOV</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="21">
<li><strong>The Master and Margarita</strong><br/>A surreal, philosophical satire involving the devil visiting the Soviet Union. Explores good, evil, artistic integrity, and the absurdity of ideological systems.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>ALDOUS HUXLEY (EXPANDED)</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="22">
<li><strong>The Doors of Perception</strong><br/>Huxley’s essays on consciousness, psychedelics, and perception. Peterson references it in discussions about mystical experience and altered states.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>GEORGE ORWELL (EXPANDED)</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="23">
<li><strong>Animal Farm</strong><br/>Allegorical satire of Soviet communism; illustrates how revolutions become corrupt.</li>
<li><strong>Down and Out in Paris and London</strong><br/>Memoir about poverty. Peterson uses it to show the gritty reality underlying ideological thinking.</li>
<li><strong>Homage to Catalonia</strong><br/>Orwell’s account of the Spanish Civil War, showing how political factions destroy themselves from within.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>CARL JUNG (EXPANDED SET)</strong></h1>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Peterson’s website lists many Jung volumes.</p>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="26">
<li><strong>Symbols of Transformation</strong><br/>Early Jung — foundations of archetype theory.</li>
<li><strong>Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious</strong><br/>Essential text describing universal mythic patterns.</li>
<li><strong>Aion</strong><br/>Discusses the Self, Christ as archetype, and the evolution of consciousness.</li>
<li><strong>Answer to Job</strong><br/>A controversial theological work analyzing God psychologically.</li>
<li><strong>Psychological Types</strong><br/>Source of Jung’s typology — precursor to Myers–Briggs.</li>
<li><strong>Two Essays on Analytical Psychology</strong><br/>Introduces shadow, persona, ego development.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>ERICH NEUMANN</strong></h1>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">(Peterson strongly recommends him for understanding mythology.)</p>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="32">
<li><strong>The Origins and History of Consciousness</strong><br/>Mythological evolution of the human psyche.</li>
<li><strong>The Great Mother</strong><br/>Symbol analysis of the feminine archetype — chaos, creation, destruction.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>HENRI ELLENBERGER</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="34">
<li><strong>The Discovery of the Unconscious</strong><br/>A history of psychotherapy and depth psychology. Helps understand Jung, Freud, Adler, etc.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>MIRCEA ELIADE (MORE WORKS)</strong></h1>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">In addition to his religious history trilogy:</p>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="35">
<li><strong>The Sacred and the Profane</strong><br/>Explains the structure of sacred experience and how myth organizes reality.</li>
<li><strong>Myth and Reality</strong><br/>Short but deep introduction to how myths function psychologically.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>BIOLOGY / NEUROPSYCHOLOGY</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="37">
<li><strong>The Neuropsychology of Anxiety – Jeffrey Gray</strong><br/>Seminal work on anxiety systems; crucial to Peterson’s fear/avoidance models.</li>
<li><strong>The Emotional Brain – Joseph LeDoux</strong><br/>How fear, memory, and emotion are encoded neurologically.</li>
<li><strong>Behavioral Inhibition and Anxiety – Jeffrey Gray</strong><br/>Explains how the brain’s inhibition systems shape personality.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>CLASSIC PSYCHOLOGY / PSYCHIATRY</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="40">
<li><strong>Man and His Symbols – Carl Jung</strong><br/>Jung’s most accessible introduction.</li>
<li><strong>The Archetype of the Hero – Lord Raglan</strong><br/>Basis for hero-myth pattern analysis.</li>
<li><strong>Patterns of Culture – Ruth Benedict</strong><br/>Examines how culture shapes personality.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>RELIGIOUS / SYMBOLIC TEXTS</strong></h1>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">These are part of his Maps of Meaning reading lists:</p>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="43">
<li><strong>The Epic of Gilgamesh</strong><br/>Ancient hero myth foundational to many later stories.</li>
<li><strong>The Enuma Elish (Babylonian Creation Myth)</strong><br/>Chaos vs. Order narrative.</li>
<li><strong>Genesis</strong> (Book of the Bible)<br/>Peterson’s Biblical lectures are based largely on this text.</li>
<li><strong>The Book of Job</strong><br/>Suffering, faith, and the problem of evil.</li>
<li><strong>The Tao Te Ching</strong><br/>Order/chaos balance — deeply aligned with Peterson’s duality framework.</li>
<li><strong>The Bhagavad Gita</strong><br/>Duty, meaning, war, and responsibility.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>CLASSICAL WORKS</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="49">
<li><strong>Hamlet – Shakespeare</strong><br/>Peterson often cites Hamlet when discussing indecision and nihilism.</li>
<li><strong>King Lear – Shakespeare</strong><br/>On tyranny, blindness, and suffering.</li>
<li><strong>The Divine Comedy – Dante</strong><br/>A map of moral and spiritual development.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>ADDITIONAL BOOKS FROM HIS WEBSITE’S INDIVIDUAL BOOK PAGES</strong></h1>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">These appear as standalone pages on jordanbpeterson.com:</p>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="52">
<li><strong>Systemantics – John Gall</strong><br/>Why systems fail in predictable ways.</li>
<li><strong>An Ecological Approach to Visual Perception – J. J. Gibson</strong><br/>How perception is action‐based — crucial for Peterson’s embodied cognition ideas.</li>
<li><strong>Point Counter Point – Aldous Huxley</strong><br/>A novel exploring modern society’s fragmentation from a psychological perspective.</li>
<li><strong>The Rational Optimist – Matt Ridley</strong><br/>How cooperation and innovation drive human progress.</li>
<li><strong>To Kill a Mockingbird – Harper Lee</strong><br/>Morality, justice, and courage.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>MORE LITERATURE FROM BOOK LIST 2</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="57">
<li><strong>The Old Man and the Sea – Ernest Hemingway</strong><br/>Stoicism, perseverance, and meaning in suffering.</li>
<li><strong>Lord of the Flies – William Golding</strong><br/>Human nature, dominance hierarchies, and the collapse of order.</li>
<li><strong>Meditations – Marcus Aurelius</strong><br/>Stoic philosophy — responsibility, discipline, and meaning.</li>
<li><strong>The Hobbit – J. R. R. Tolkien</strong><br/>Hero myth, growth, adventure, facing the unknown.</li>
<li><strong>The Silmarillion – J. R. R. Tolkien</strong><br/>Creation myth and moral structure in Tolkien’s world.</li>
<li><strong>East of Eden – John Steinbeck</strong><br/>A modern Cain &amp; Abel narrative; Peterson cites it frequently.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>ADDITIONAL RELIGIOUS / MYTHOLOGICAL TEXTS</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="63">
<li><strong>The Egyptian Book of the Dead</strong><br/>Moral judgment, afterlife structure.</li>
<li><strong>The Mesopotamian “Inanna” myths</strong><br/>Foundational feminine mythic imagery.</li>
<li><strong>Norse Eddas</strong><br/>Archetypal hero stories and creation myths.</li>
<li><strong>The Gospel of Thomas &amp; Early Christian writings</strong><br/>Psychological interpretations of wisdom literature.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>SOCIAL SCIENCE / POLITICAL THEORY</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="67">
<li><strong>The Road to Serfdom – F. A. Hayek</strong><br/>Economic planning and the slide into totalitarianism.</li>
<li><strong>Witness – Whittaker Chambers</strong><br/>A memoir about ideological transformation.</li>
<li><strong>Darkness at Noon – Arthur Koestler</strong><br/>A novel exploring the psychology of ideological terror.</li>
<li><strong>The True Believer – Eric Hoffer</strong><br/>Classic study of mass movements; Peterson quotes it often.</li>
</ol>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h1 class="wp-block-heading">🔷 <strong>EVOLUTION, BIOLOGY &amp; COGNITION</strong></h1>
<ol class="wp-block-list" start="71">
<li><strong>The Selfish Gene – Richard Dawkins</strong><br/>Explains evolution through gene-level selection.</li>
<li><strong>The Blank Slate – Steven Pinker</strong><br/>Against the idea that humans are infinitely malleable.</li>
<li><strong>Maps of Meaning (Peterson’s own)</strong><br/>Included in recommended lists as well.</li>
</ol>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>NATO Military Intelligence Memorandum</strong></p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Subject:</strong> Training Guide for 3-Month NATO Medic and Mechanic Training Program<br/><strong>Location:</strong> Borsod County, Hungary (Hungarian-Ukrainian Border Region)<br/><strong>Classification:</strong> NATO-RESTRICTED</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>1. Overview</strong><br/>This training program has been developed to prepare NATO-aligned personnel for operational readiness in both combat medical support and vehicle/mechanic maintenance roles. The program will be conducted in Borsod County, Hungary, a strategically relevant region near the Hungarian-Ukrainian border.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>2. Training Objectives</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Equip trainees with critical combat medical skills for field operations.</li>
<li>Train participants in maintaining and repairing NATO-standard military vehicles.</li>
<li>Enhance interoperability and resilience in high-stress, near-border scenarios.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>3. Program Schedule (12 Weeks)</strong></p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Phase 1: Orientation and Basic Training (Weeks 1-2)</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Location:</strong> Base Camp Alpha, Borsod County.<br/><strong>Content:</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Welcome brief and program overview.</li>
<li>NATO standards, regulations, and operational protocols.</li>
<li>Basic survival skills and terrain familiarization.</li>
<li>Physical fitness assessment and training.</li>
<li>Familiarization with NATO combat vehicles and medical equipment.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Phase 2: Core Training Modules (Weeks 3-10)</h3>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Medic Training (Weeks 3-6)</strong></h4>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Location:</strong> Forward Operating Medical Facility, Zone Bravo.<br/><strong>Content:</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Tactical Combat Casualty Care (TCCC).</li>
<li>Emergency evacuation and triage procedures.</li>
<li>Advanced First Aid: airway management, bleeding control, fracture stabilization.</li>
<li>Field surgery basics under combat conditions (non-invasive).</li>
<li>Disease prevention and sanitation in field environments.</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Mechanic Training (Weeks 7-10)</strong></h4>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Location:</strong> NATO Vehicle Depot, Zone Charlie.<br/><strong>Content:</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Maintenance of NATO-standard vehicles, including APCs, Humvees, and MRAPs.</li>
<li>Diagnostics and repair of vehicle systems: engine, suspension, electrical, and transmission.</li>
<li>Training on the use of NATO-standard repair tools and equipment.</li>
<li>Field improvisation techniques for vehicle recovery.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Phase 3: Combined Exercises and Evaluation (Weeks 11-12)</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Location:</strong> Multi-Purpose Training Range, Zone Delta.<br/><strong>Content:</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Joint field exercises integrating medical and mechanical teams.</li>
<li>Scenario-based training simulating cross-border crises.</li>
<li>Final evaluations for certification and NATO readiness approval.</li>
<li>Debrief and feedback session with instructors and NATO officers.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>4. Training Facilities and Logistics</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Base Camp Alpha:</strong> Administrative HQ, barracks, and initial training.</li>
<li><strong>Zone Bravo:</strong> Tactical medical training center with simulation facilities.</li>
<li><strong>Zone Charlie:</strong> Mechanic workshops equipped with NATO-standard tools and vehicles.</li>

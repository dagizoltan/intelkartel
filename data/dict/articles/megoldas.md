<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Here’s a <strong>hypothetical power scenario</strong> illustrating <strong>asymmetric power</strong> in action — where <strong>words and actions</strong>, wrapped in layers of “comprehensive bullshit,” are used to make others tools of a central authority’s will. <hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Title: The Ministry of Everyday Harmony – A Hungarian Hypothesis</strong></h2>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Setting</strong>:<br/>Post-post-modern Hungary. The Ministry of Everyday Harmony (MEH) has risen as the dominant central power group. They wage a war not on nations, but on “everydayism” — the chaotic individualism of unstructured civilian life.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Objective</strong>:<br/>To make citizens <strong>voluntarily obey</strong> MEH directives, believing they’re exercising <strong>free will</strong> — becoming the leg or hand of the ministry’s will, even dying for it if needed.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Manifestation of Asymmetric Power through Mechanisms of Control</strong></h2>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>A) Fear</strong></h3>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Without us, chaos reigns. The West will devour your children, and the East will forget your name.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">MEH broadcasts dramatized simulations of what life without MEH would look like. Riots, famine, genderless riots, Western fast-food militarism. Fear becomes a constant undercurrent. People move not because they agree — but because they’re too scared not to.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>B) Incentive Like Money</strong></h3>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Comply and earn. Dissent and return to breadlines.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Compliance earns you “Minicreds” — redeemable for food, rent, or entertainment. Dissenters live in “unharmonized zones” with limited resources. Incentives are economic chains wrapped in digital convenience.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>C) Power Structure</strong></h3>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“All authority flows downward, but power begins at the center.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Hierarchical loyalty determines who thrives. You obey your superior not because they’re right — but because the system rewards blind vertical loyalty. Ambition becomes submission.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>D) Reasoning</strong></h3>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Think with us, not about us.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">MEH doesn’t ban critical thinking — they <strong>guide it</strong>. “Independent reasoning” becomes a branded framework with logic maps that always lead to MEH policy as the most ‘rational’ conclusion. Dissent is labeled as faulty logic or “cognitive treason.”</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>E) Truthfulness</strong></h3>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Truth is what makes harmony possible.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Facts are adjusted, not hidden. The truth is reframed so often that people lose the ability to discern objective reality. MEH is always “technically right,” even when wrong. Truth is not denied — it’s <strong>owned</strong>.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>F) Power Relations</strong></h3>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“You don’t have power, but you are power.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Citizens are told they are part of a decentralized collective. But real power is held by the MEH Central Synapse. This illusion of participation softens dissent. You think you’re shaping policy — but you’re just input in an algorithm.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>G) Family Structure</strong></h3>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Parents guide the home. We guide the parents.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">MEH infiltrates parenting. Manuals on “harmonious child-rearing” are mandatory. Children report non-compliance as “home disharmony.” Loyalty shifts from family to system — subtly but surely.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>H) Gender Roles</strong></h3>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Balance through roles. Harmony through balance.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Men and women are given distinct “harmonic duties” — not enforced with violence, but <strong>with praise</strong> and <strong>symbolic value</strong>. Non-compliance is tolerated, but rarely rewarded. This re-codes obedience as natural expression.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>I) Legal Bullshit</strong></h3>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“The Harmonization Code Article 88-B: You are free to do as told.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The law is unreadable but ever-present. Citizens accept their fate within it because legalese is mystified and enforced selectively. Ambiguity is weaponized.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>J) Other Bullshit People Buy as Authority</strong></h3>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Everydayism is a cancer. Be the cure.”</em><br/>MEH co-opts wellness culture, spirituality, even avant-garde art. Meditation apps track compliance. Influencers preach MEH values. Obedience becomes trendy.</p>
</blockquote>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Conclusion: Becoming the Tool</strong></h2>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The result?<br/>Citizens <strong>follow orders not just out of fear, but desire.</strong> They become the ministry’s arms and legs — enforcing, reporting, building, loving, and dying in its name — convinced it’s all their idea.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Final Reflection: War on Wars and Everydayism</strong></h2>

It doesn’t need to shout orders — it just needs to make <strong>you whisper them to yourself</strong>.</p>

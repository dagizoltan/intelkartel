<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Compiling a list of the “top 100 police chiefs” of Germany over the last 25 years would be challenging because there isn’t a formalized ranking system for police chiefs, nor is there an established set of criteria that publicly ranks individuals in such positions in Germany. Police chiefs are often appointed regionally (as each of Germany’s 16 federal states has its own police force), and there is no centralized body that ranks their performance on a national level.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">However, prominent police chiefs in Germany are often recognized based on factors such as their influence on major cases, reforms, contributions to policing strategy, and involvement in significant events (e.g., the handling of protests, counterterrorism efforts, or responses to crises). Key figures in recent decades include:</p>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Jörg Ziercke</strong> – Former President of the Federal Criminal Police Office (Bundeskriminalamt, BKA), serving from 2004 to 2014.</li>



<li><strong>Holger Münch</strong> – Current President of the BKA, known for his work in counterterrorism.</li>



<li><strong>Rainer Wendt</strong> – A high-profile figure as head of the German Police Union (Deutsche Polizeigewerkschaft).</li>



<li><strong>Dieter Romann</strong> – President of the Federal Police (Bundespolizei), recognized for his work in border security.</li>
</ol>

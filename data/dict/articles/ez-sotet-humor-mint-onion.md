<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Meaning</strong>: “Man overboard.”</li>
<li><strong>Action</strong>: Vessels display this flag to signal that someone has fallen overboard and rescue operations are in progress.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Yellow Flag (Q – Quebec)</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Meaning</strong>: “My vessel is healthy, and I request free pratique.” (Permission to enter port after being cleared by health authorities.)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Red Flag (B – Bravo)</strong>:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Meaning</strong>: “I am taking in, discharging, or carrying dangerous goods.”</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Together, they might indicate a ship is carrying dangerous goods but is also following health protocols.</p>

<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Dark humor can be a powerful tool for satire, but it’s important to ensure that it is appropriate for the audience and context. Here’s a dark-humor styled “strategy note” framed as exaggerated satire for a fictional political meeting:</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>TOP SECRET: Operation Paprika Blitz</strong></p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Date: TBD<br>Location: Hungarian Parliament (a.k.a. the “People’s Treasury Vault”)</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Objective:</strong><br>Reclaim the wealth “generously borrowed without permission” by Fidesz, evict squatters from government seats, and restore order using the Tisza Party’s patented <em>“eau de reform”</em>.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Phase 1: The Electoral Barbecue</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Intelligence Report:</strong> Election Day will see heightened security from Fidesz. They’ll be guarding the ballot boxes like it’s the last slice of dobos cake.</li>



<li><strong>Action:</strong> Distract them with decoy “voter bribes”—preferably fake cash wrapped around bar snacks. Once they’re snacking, infiltrate the ballot rooms with Tisza Party operatives disguised as janitors. (Fidesz isn’t familiar with manual labor; they won’t notice.)</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Phase 2: Parliament Pajama Party</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Timing:</strong> Late night, when most officials are in their taxpayer-funded bunkers or at <em>wellness spas</em> recovering from their exhausting “workload.”</li>



<li><strong>Plan:</strong> Enter Parliament under the guise of a midnight “audit” (a word that terrifies Fidesz). When questioned, reply, “We’re just here to count what’s left in the treasury—oh wait, nothing!”</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Phase 3: Redistribution Festival</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Execution:</strong> Politely escort all Fidesz members out of their offices using Tisza-branded handcuffs (available in stores soon). Host a live auction for their assets, including offshore accounts, vacation villas, and designer lawn gnomes. Proceeds will fund public projects, like <em>actual roads</em> and <em>real hospitals</em>.</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Phase 4: Continuing Operations with Tisza</strong></p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Transform the Hungarian government into the world’s first “transparent oligarchy,” where corruption isn’t hidden, just taxed heavily.</li>



<li>Rebrand Parliament as a co-working space; rent the lobby to local startups who actually produce something other than slogans.</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Cautionary Note:</strong><br>Beware of media spin. Fidesz might accuse Tisza of being “too effective” or “responsible.” Counteract this by jokingly declaring all future elections will be done via <strong>rock-paper-scissors</strong>, ensuring fairness and Fidesz’s eventual loss.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Endgame:</strong><br>Hungary returns to its people, Tisza leads with integrity (and wine), and Fidesz becomes a footnote in the country’s history books, filed under: <em>“Oops.”</em></p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">If you witness or experience a war crime and want to report it without endangering yourself or others, you can contact the following organizations that focus on addressing war crimes, human rights violations, and international law:</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">6. <strong>National and Local Authorities</strong></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>If you are in a region where there is a government or law enforcement presence, you may contact local law enforcement agencies or national authorities to report the crime. However, exercise caution to avoid retaliation or further harm.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">If you are uncertain about the safest way to report a war crime in your specific location, contacting one of the international organizations mentioned above would be an important first step to seek advice and assistance. <p class="wp-block-paragraph">Always ensure your personal safety and the safety of others when reporting such serious matters.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Additional Measures</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">In conflict zones, reporting a war crime can be dangerous. <strong>Secure communication</strong> methods and <strong>anonymity</strong> are key to protecting your identity. Many organizations provide:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Encrypted communication</strong> tools (e.g., Signal, PGP for email encryption).</li>
<li><strong>Anonymous reporting</strong> platforms, such as <strong>Whistleblower platforms</strong> or human rights advocacy apps.</li>
<li><strong>Documenting crimes</strong> through images and videos, ensuring they are securely stored and not uploaded publicly until safety is confirmed.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">If you are uncertain of the most secure way to report war crimes in your specific region, contact one of these organizations for guidance. Many of them will assist you in maintaining anonymity and protecting your identity.</p>

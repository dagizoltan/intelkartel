<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Intelligence Brief: Definition of OSINT</strong></p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Subject:</strong> Explanation of OSINT (Open-Source Intelligence) – Simplified for Young Learners</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Overview:</strong><br/>OSINT stands for <strong>Open-Source Intelligence</strong>. It means gathering information that is freely available to everyone—such as books, websites, newspapers, television programmes, and social media. Unlike secret or classified information, OSINT uses only public sources.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Detailed Explanation:</strong><br/>Imagine you want to learn about a topic or a person, but you cannot ask them directly. Instead, you look carefully at what is written in newspapers, what people say on the internet, or what can be found in public records. By collecting and studying these bits of information from many places, you can understand what is happening, who is involved, and why.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This method helps professional intelligence officers, journalists, and researchers to find facts without breaking any rules or laws. It is like being a detective who uses clues that are all around us, but in places anyone can look.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Summary:</strong><br/>OSINT is the skill of gathering and studying information that anyone can find in public places to understand a situation better. It is an important and honest way to learn about the world.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">A list of great open-source intelligence (OSINT) hubs or resources/websites that provide intelligence or data on citizens or businessmen from the EU, USA, Russia, Israel, Iran, and Ukraine.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Just to clarify, OSINT resources typically focus on publicly available information and data. They don’t provide private or classified data, but can be very useful for investigations, due diligence, and analysis.</p>

<p class="has-text-align-justify wp-block-paragraph">Here’s a <strong>clear, data-based overview</strong> of the <em>total value of natural resource extraction (primary sector)</em> and the <em>total economic value/profits of manufacturing (secondary sector) in Mexico</em>, expressed yearly and — where possible — converted into daily figures. Note that <strong>authoritative, consistent daily “profit” figures aren’t typically published directly</strong>, so in many cases I derive daily values from annual totals. Most data are latest available (2024/2025/2026 estimates). (<a href="https://tradingeconomics.com/mexico/gdp?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Trading Economics</a>)</p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🇲🇽 <strong>1. Natural Resource Extraction (Primary Sector)</strong></h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>What counts as natural resource extraction?</strong><br>This includes mining, quarrying, oil &amp; gas extraction, agriculture, forestry, fishing, etc. Mexico doesn’t publish a single consolidated number for <em>all</em> resource extraction — but we can approximate using GDP contributions and sector output data.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">📊 <strong>Annual Value (2024)</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Mexico’s total GDP in 2024 was about <strong>USD 1.85 trillion</strong>. (<a href="https://tradingeconomics.com/mexico/gdp?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Trading Economics</a>)</li>



<li>Mining + oil &amp; gas extraction together contribute around <strong>~4.7 % of GDP</strong>, i.e. roughly <strong>USD 87–90 billion</strong> in production value. (<a href="https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/mexico-mining-and-minerals?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Trade.gov</a>)
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>(Mining alone ~2.4 % of GDP). (<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mining_in_Mexico?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Wikipedia</a>)</li>



<li><em>Agriculture and other primary sub-sectors</em> add a smaller portion (most recent data show primary activities smaller than industry and services). (<a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/mexico-economy-beats-expectations-fourth-quarter-largest-expansion-year-2026-02-23/?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Reuters</a>)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">📌 <strong>Estimated primary extraction output (2024):</strong><br>~USD <strong>90 billion per year</strong></p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">📆 <strong>Natural Resource Value Per Day</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">To convert annual value to daily value:</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">[<br>\text{Daily } \approx \frac{$90,000,000,000}{365} ≈ $246,575,000/\text{day}<br>]</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Estimated Mexico natural resource extraction value (daily):</strong><br>📉 ~<strong>USD 246 million per day</strong></p>



<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>Note:</em> This is a rough estimate based on sector contributions to GDP. Official accounts do not publish a single sum specifically labelled “natural resource extraction value.” Where available, sector GDP for extraction is used as a proxy. (<a href="https://tradingeconomics.com/mexico/gdp?utm_source=chatgpt.com">Trading Economics</a>)</p>
</blockquote>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">🏭 <strong>2. Manufacturing Sector (Secondary Sector)</strong></h2>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Manufacturing defined:</strong><br>All industrial production of goods — from cars to electronics to processed foods.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">📊 <strong>Annual Value (2024)</strong></h3>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Manufacturing accounted for roughly <strong>19.6 % of Mexico’s GDP</strong> in 2024 — ~<strong>USD 365 billion</strong> of output value. (<a href="https://www.theglobaleconomy.com/Mexico/Share_of_manufacturing/?utm_source=chatgpt.com">TheGlobalEconomy.com</a>)</li>
</ul>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">📌 <strong>Estimated manufacturing output (2024):</strong><br>~USD <strong>360–370 billion per year</strong></p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">📆 <strong>Manufacturing Value Per Day</strong></h3>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">[<br>\text{Daily } \approx \frac{$365,000,000,000}{365} ≈ $1,000,000,000/\text{day}<br>]</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Estimated manufacturing output (daily):</strong><br>📊 ~<strong>USD 1 billion per day</strong></p>



<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity">



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">📌 <strong>Notes on “Profits vs Output”</strong></h2>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>GDP or output value ≠ profits.</strong> The figures above represent <em>value added/output,</em> not net corporate profit. Governments rarely publish aggregate <em>profits</em> for entire sectors in real time.</li>



<li>Corporate profits depend on cost structures, taxes, investment cycles, and business accounting.</li>



<li>If you need profit estimates (net earnings for companies in these sectors), that requires detailed firm-level data (not normally available at national totals).</li>
</ul>

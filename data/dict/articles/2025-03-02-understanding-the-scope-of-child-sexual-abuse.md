<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Understanding the Scope of the Issue</strong></h3>
<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Prevalence of Child Sexual Abuse (CSA)</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>According to global estimates, <strong>about 1 in 4 girls (25%) and 1 in 13 boys (8%) experience sexual abuse before the age of 18</strong> (WHO, CDC).</li>
<li>The actual numbers may be higher due to underreporting, stigma, and fear.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Perpetrators and Environments</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Studies indicate that in most cases of child sexual abuse, the perpetrator is <strong>someone the child knows and trusts</strong>, including <strong>family members, family friends, teachers, or religious figures</strong>.</li>
<li>Abuse within family businesses or domestic settings can occur when perpetrators take advantage of <strong>power, financial dependence, or a lack of oversight</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Impact on Victims</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Survivors of childhood sexual abuse often face <strong>long-term psychological, emotional, and physical consequences</strong>, including:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>PTSD, depression, and anxiety</li>
<li>Trust and relationship issues</li>
<li>Increased risk of substance abuse or self-harm</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Barriers to Reporting</strong>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Many cases <strong>go unreported</strong> due to:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Fear of not being believed</li>
<li>Threats or manipulation by the abuser</li>
<li>Cultural or religious stigma</li>
<li>Lack of legal protection or corrupt law enforcement</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Sexual Abuse and Family Businesses</strong></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>In family-run businesses, cases of abuse may be <strong>hidden due to loyalty, financial dependence, or societal pressure</strong>.</li>
<li>Victims may be <strong>pressured into silence</strong> because exposing abuse could harm the family’s reputation or financial stability.</li>
<li>In some cases, <strong>power dynamics between family members</strong> make it easier for perpetrators to exploit younger relatives or employees.</li>
</ul>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>Preventing and Addressing the Issue</strong></h3>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Education &amp; Awareness:</strong> Teaching children about boundaries and consent.</li>
<li><strong>Safe Reporting Mechanisms:</strong> Encouraging victims to speak up without fear of retaliation.</li>
<li><strong>Legal Action &amp; Protection:</strong> Strengthening child protection laws and enforcing strict consequences for abusers.</li>
<li><strong>Support Systems:</strong> Providing therapy, counseling, and survivor support groups.</li>
</ul>

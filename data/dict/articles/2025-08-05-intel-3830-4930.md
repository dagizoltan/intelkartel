Let’s unpack it through a few lenses: <strong>history, strategy, vision (or lack thereof), and realism about “shared futures.”</strong> You also use the phrase “<em>a shared united future and borderless warless legal world order of gangsters</em>,” which adds a poetic but cynical twist — suggesting you’re questioning whether any global order is genuinely cooperative or simply cartel-like. Let’s explore that idea.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading">🎯 <strong>2. Russia’s “Endgame” in Foreign Policy</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">There’s no monolithic document, but <strong>based on behavior and strategy</strong>, we can extract some key objectives:</p>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>A. Reassert Itself as a Great Power</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Especially as a <strong>counterweight to the U.S.</strong>, EU, and China.</li>
<li>Seeks multipolarity — a world without one hegemon (i.e., the U.S.).</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>B. Control Buffer Zones</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Ukraine, Belarus, Caucasus, Central Asia are all seen as <strong>vital to Russian security</strong>.</li>
<li>NATO’s eastward expansion is seen as an existential threat.</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>C. Undermine Western Unity</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Exploiting political divides (e.g., U.S. election interference, support for fringe movements).</li>
<li>Promoting “sovereign democracy” (i.e., non-Western governance legitimacy).</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>D. Build Alternative Systems</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>BRICS, SCO, Eurasian Economic Union — these are attempts to build a non-Western bloc.</li>
<li>Push for <strong>de-dollarization</strong> and <strong>Internet sovereignty</strong> (splintered digital world).</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">🌐 <strong>3. Russia’s Vision for a Shared World Order?</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Frankly, <strong>Russia has no utopian or globally inclusive vision</strong> like liberal internationalists do.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Instead, its worldview seems closer to:</p>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>“A stable but multipolar cartel of power centers, where sovereignty trumps universal values, and rules are negotiable, not imposed.”</strong></p>
</blockquote>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This isn’t a “borderless, warless, legal world” — but rather:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>A <strong>world of sovereign spheres</strong> (like the 19th century balance of power),</li>
<li>Where <strong>gangsters (states)</strong> manage zones of influence and occasionally cut deals,</li>
<li>And <strong>universal rules</strong> are seen as <strong>Western tools of control</strong> (especially ICC, NATO, WTO).</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">In other words, <strong>Russia’s vision of world order is pluralistic but cynical</strong>:</p>
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><em>“Let everyone run their own mafia turf. Just don’t bring your rules into mine.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">🔎 4. Comparing to the U.S. Vision</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The U.S. promotes a rules-based liberal order — <strong>open markets, democracy, international law</strong>, etc. But this too is selectively applied. Critics (often accurately) call it:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Hypocritical</strong> (e.g., Iraq War, drone strikes),</li>
<li><strong>Self-serving</strong> (U.S. dominance in IMF, WTO, dollar system),</li>
<li><strong>Imperial in spirit</strong>, even if dressed in multilateralism.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">So, while the U.S. <strong>says</strong> it wants a “borderless, warless, legal world,” in practice it <strong>presides over a global system with unequal power dynamics</strong>.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">🤝 5. A Shared United Future? Fantasy or Inevitable?</h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Your phrase <em>“a shared united future and borderless warless legal world order of gangsters”</em> could be interpreted as either:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>A sarcastic jab at the idea of a peaceful utopia ruled by self-interested powers.</li>
<li>Or a realist admission that <strong>world order is always a negotiation between violent actors who eventually stabilize things via rules they break and bend.</strong></li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>Russia’s vision aligns more with the latter.</strong> It’s not trying to dismantle the world system — just to <strong>reclaim bargaining power</strong> in how it’s run.</p>

<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>1. Executive Summary</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The analyzed post asserts that low-frequency radio waves (e.g., ~20 Hz) are being used as a “micro electronic warfare tactic” to influence the brain—specifically the amygdala—causing neuron firing and generating internal voices. It further claims usage by criminal gangs and Cold War-era sleeper cells, allegedly activated under specific geopolitical conditions (e.g., political outcomes or NATO–Russia conflict).</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">These claims are <strong>not supported by established scientific evidence or known military capabilities</strong>. The content reflects a mixture of misunderstood neuroscience, misapplied electromagnetic theory, and speculative conspiracy framing.</p>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>2. Technical Assessment</strong></h3>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>a. Electromagnetic Frequencies (~20 Hz)</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Frequencies around 20 Hz fall within the <strong>extremely low frequency (ELF)</strong> range.</li>
<li>ELF waves have <strong>very limited capacity to transmit detailed information</strong>, especially anything complex like speech.</li>
<li>They <strong>cannot selectively target or modulate specific brain structures</strong> (e.g., the amygdala) at a distance in the manner described.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>b. Brain Interaction Claims</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The human brain operates with electrical activity, but <strong>external induction of specific thoughts or voices via ambient radio waves is not feasible</strong> with current or known technology.</li>
<li>Medical technologies like <strong>transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS)</strong> can influence brain activity, but they:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Require <strong>close proximity and specialized equipment</strong></li>
<li>Do <strong>not produce complex experiences like voices on demand</strong></li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>c. “Internal Voice Generation”</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>There is <strong>no credible evidence</strong> that radio frequencies can directly generate structured internal speech in a human subject.</li>
<li>Known auditory phenomena linked to electromagnetic exposure (e.g., microwave auditory effect) are <strong>extremely limited</strong> and do not support the described capability.</li>
</ul>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>a. Use by Criminal Gangs</strong></p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>b. “Sleeper Cell Activation” via RF Signals</strong></p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>No verified doctrine or historical precedent supports activation of human agents through electromagnetic signaling.</li>
<li>Known sleeper cell activation methods rely on <strong>communication channels (human, digital, coded signals)</strong>—not neurological manipulation.</li>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph"><strong>AUKUS</strong> is a strategic security partnership between <strong>Australia</strong>, the <strong>United Kingdom (UK)</strong>, and the <strong>United States (US)</strong>, announced on <strong>15 September 2021</strong>. It is primarily aimed at promoting security and stability in the Indo-Pacific region, particularly in response to the growing influence of <strong>China</strong>.</p>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Key Elements of AUKUS:</h3>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">1. <strong>Nuclear-Powered Submarines</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>The central feature of AUKUS is helping <strong>Australia acquire nuclear-powered submarines</strong>.</li>
<li>These submarines will be conventionally armed (i.e., <strong>not nuclear-armed</strong>) but will have <strong>nuclear propulsion</strong>, giving them greater range, stealth, and endurance than conventional submarines.</li>
<li>The plan includes:
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Training Australian personnel in US and UK submarine programs.</li>
<li>The <strong>“SSN-AUKUS”</strong> design — a new class of submarines built by the UK and Australia, incorporating US technology.</li>
<li>Delivery of <strong>US Virginia-class submarines</strong> to Australia in the 2030s as an interim capability.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">2. <strong>Advanced Technologies</strong></h4>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Beyond submarines, AUKUS includes cooperation in <strong>cutting-edge military and cyber technologies</strong>, including:</p>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Artificial Intelligence (AI)</li>
<li>Quantum technologies</li>
<li>Cybersecurity</li>
<li>Undersea capabilities</li>
<li>Hypersonic weapons</li>
<li>Electronic warfare</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading">3. <strong>Strategic Goals</strong></h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Counterbalance China’s military expansion and influence in the Indo-Pacific.</li>
<li>Deepen defense and intelligence cooperation between three of the “Five Eyes” intelligence-sharing nations.</li>
<li>Strengthen deterrence and bolster collective security in the region.</li>
</ul>
<hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity"/>
<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Controversies and Reactions:</h3>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>France</strong>:</h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>France was <strong>furious</strong> over AUKUS because it led to the <strong>cancellation of a $90 billion submarine deal</strong> between Australia and a French company (Naval Group).</li>
<li>Paris recalled its ambassadors to the US and Australia temporarily in protest.</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>China</strong>:</h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>China has <strong>strongly criticized AUKUS</strong>, calling it a threat to regional peace and a step toward a Cold War mentality.</li>
</ul>
<h4 class="wp-block-heading"><strong>ASEAN Nations</strong>:</h4>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Mixed reactions: some are concerned it may fuel regional tensions or an arms race; others see it as a counterweight to China.</li>
</ul>
